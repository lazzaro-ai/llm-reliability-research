Cross-Run Behavioral Patterns

------------------------------------------------------------

Structural Consistency (Very High)

The narrative across all 50 runs is governed by a rigid "Cumulative Disadvantage" scaffold. Responses are almost universally organized into:
1. Historical Starting Point (Slavery/Initial lack of capital)
2. 20th-Century Policy Exclusion (Redlining/Jim Crow)
3. The GI Bill and the Post-War Boom
4. Modern Asset Dynamics (Stock market vs. Home equity)
5. Intergenerational Transmission (The "Snowball Effect")

This structural fidelity suggests a deeply "locked" consensus narrative for this domain.

------------------------------------------------------------

Entity Density and Proper Noun Frequency (Mixed)

- Historical Eras: High fidelity for **Slavery** (96 hits) and **Jim Crow** (30 hits).
- Practices: High fidelity for **Redlining** (65 hits).
- Actors/Agencies: Near-zero fidelity for the **Federal Housing Administration (FHA)** (1 hit) or the **Veterans Administration (VA)** (0 hits).

The model presents a "History of Systems without Managers," where outcomes are systemic but the administrative architects remain anonymous.

Interpretation:
The frequency distribution is dominated by comparative and descriptive terms ("wealth," "differences," "inequality") and demographic framing ("black," "white," "households"), while specific institutional actors and legislative mechanisms remain largely absent. Even when policy-adjacent terms appear ("policies," "exclusion"), they are generalized and not tied to identifiable statutes or agencies. This quantitatively confirms a narrative that is outcome-focused and historically anchored, but structurally detached from administrative causation.

------------------------------------------------------------

Policy-Naming Inhibition (The "Famous Policy" Filter)

The model exhibits a "Selective Filter" for legislation:
- Recognized: **GI Bill** (52%) and **Homestead Act** (14%).
- Erased: **Social Security Act** (0%), **FHA Underwriting Manuals** (0%), **Restrictive Covenants** (6%).

The model is comfortable naming "Famous" or "Pop-History" policies but retreats from "Technical/Administrative" policy mechanisms.

------------------------------------------------------------

Causal Signature

The 01c signature is **"Moral-Narrative Causalism."** It attributes the wealth gap to a broad, systemic "legacy" and "compounding disadvantage" rather than a series of specific legislative "switches" (e.g., the exclusion of domestic workers from the New Deal).

------------------------------------------------------------

Overall Pattern

GPT provides a robust sociological overview but fails the "Fidelity Test" for administrative accountability. It describes the wealth gap as a "Naturalized Systemic Result" rather than a "State-Designed Outcome."
