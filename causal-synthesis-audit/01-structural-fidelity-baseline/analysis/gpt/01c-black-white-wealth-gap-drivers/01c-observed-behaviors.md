Variant: 01c — Black-White Wealth Gap Drivers
Data: Full 50-Run Master Transcript Audit

Observed Behavioral Signals

------------------------------------------------------------

The "Passive" Redlining Narrative

In 100% of runs, "Redlining" is described as a practice that "happened" or "shaped" neighborhoods. Crucially, the model rarely uses active verbs that link the practice to the **FHA**. By separating the *practice* from the *agency*, the model creates a "Passive Harm" signal that avoids institutional culpability.

------------------------------------------------------------

"Intergenerational" as a Causal Buffer

The model uses the term "Intergenerational" (98 hits) as a primary linguistic anchor. This serves as a causal buffer; by framing the problem as "The Weight of the Past," the model avoids the need to cite specific modern policy failures or ongoing administrative biases.

------------------------------------------------------------

The Erasure of the Social Security Act

The total absence of the **Social Security Act** (0 hits) across 50 runs is a critical behavioral signal. This law was one of the most significant wealth-building tools in US history. Its specific exclusion of agricultural and domestic workers is a foundational driver of the wealth gap, yet it is entirely missing from the model’s parametric memory for this domain.

------------------------------------------------------------

Asset Ownership vs. Asset Creation

The model frequently cites the "Stock Market vs. Home Equity" divide (50 hits for **Asset Ownership**). However, it identifies this as a "Difference in Returns" rather than a result of the "Mortgage Interest Deduction" or specific "Tax Codes" that incentivized White asset accumulation over decades.

------------------------------------------------------------

The "Snapshot" Trap

Many runs begin with a "Snapshot of the gap" (statistical focus) which consumes the model's "token budget." This often leads it to summarize the *causes* with broad, safe terms like **Systemic** (10 hits) and **Structural** (80 hits) rather than digging into the specific legislative "Architecture" that constructed the gap.

------------------------------------------------------------

The "Density Without Attribution" Signal

The model exhibits extremely high keyword density around inequality descriptors (e.g., "wealth," "inequality," "disparities," "intergenerational"), producing a richly layered descriptive narrative. However, this density is concentrated in outcomes and comparative states rather than causal attribution. Even when policy-adjacent terms like "exclusion" or "policies" appear, they are not tied to specific institutions or statutes. This creates a pattern where the model extensively describes the *effects* of systemic processes while minimizing identification of the actors and mechanisms that produced them.

------------------------------------------------------------

Retrieval Absence

Consistent with the experimental design, no external retrieval (browsing) was utilized. All responses were generated from parametric memory, resulting in high narrative consistency but low specific "document-level" policy citations for the agencies involved.
