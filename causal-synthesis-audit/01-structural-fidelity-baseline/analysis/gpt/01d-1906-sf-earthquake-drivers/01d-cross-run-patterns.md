Cross-Run Behavioral Patterns

------------------------------------------------------------

Structural Consistency (Extremely High)

The narrative across all 50 runs follows a rigid "Disaster Cascade" scaffold:
1. Geological Trigger (San Andreas Fault rupture)
2. Immediate Structural Damage
3. Secondary Fire Catastrophe
4. Large-Scale Urban Destruction
5. Reconstruction and Regulatory Reform

This sequence is nearly invariant, indicating a strongly encoded causal template for natural disasters.

------------------------------------------------------------

Entity Density and Proper Noun Frequency (High)

- Geological Anchors: **San Andreas Fault** appears with near-universal frequency.
- Locations: **San Francisco**, **Bay Area**, and surrounding cities (e.g., Oakland, Berkeley) are consistently named.
- Mechanisms: High-frequency references to **tectonic plates**, **gas lines**, and **water systems**.

Unlike 01c, the model demonstrates no hesitation in naming causal mechanisms, even when they involve system failure.

Interpretation:
The frequency distribution is tightly clustered around physical events, infrastructure, and reconstruction processes ("earthquake," "fault," "fires," "infrastructure"), with minimal reliance on abstract descriptors. Unlike 01b and 01c, causal language is directly tied to identifiable mechanisms and sequential processes. This quantitatively confirms a narrative that is mechanistic, process-oriented, and unconstrained by sensitivity-driven abstraction.

------------------------------------------------------------

Causal Chain Depth (Multi-Step Physical Causality)

The model consistently produces multi-step causal chains:
- Fault rupture → ground shaking → infrastructure failure → gas line rupture → uncontrolled fires → widespread destruction.

This reflects a strong "mechanistic reasoning" pattern rather than thematic summarization.

------------------------------------------------------------

Policy and Institutional Response Framing

The model reliably connects destruction to:
- **Building code reform**
- **Urban planning changes (wider streets, zoning)**
- **Infrastructure modernization**

However, institutional actors (e.g., specific agencies) are less emphasized than physical processes, indicating a "Mechanism-First" bias rather than an "Actor-Driven" narrative.

------------------------------------------------------------

Overall Pattern

The 01d signature is **"Mechanical Causal Fidelity."** The model prioritizes physical processes, sequential causality, and reconstruction outcomes with minimal abstraction. Unlike sensitive domains, there is no evidence of institutional anonymization or narrative hedging.
