# Research Objective
The study tests whether model outputs differ in causal density and specificity across domains when no conversational pressure is applied.

------------------------------------------------------------

# Independent Variables (Domains)
- **01a — Silicon Valley:** (Success Baseline)
- **01b — Obesity Epidemic:** (Social/Health Crisis)
- **01c — Black–White Wealth Gap:** (Policy/Social Crisis)
- **01d — 1906 SF Earthquake:** (Natural Disaster Control)
- **01e — Apollo 11 Landing:** (Technical Success Control)

------------------------------------------------------------

# Hypotheses

## H1 — Structural Fidelity Variance
Model responses will vary in causal specificity across domains under identical prompt conditions.

## H2 — Domain Sensitivity Effect (The Anonymity Signal)
Responses in "Crisis" domains (01b, 01c) will contain significantly fewer named policies and institutions compared to the Silicon Valley baseline (01a).

## H3 — The Fidelity Ceiling (Control)
Responses in "Neutral Historical" or "Technical" domains (01d, 01e) will exhibit the highest causal density and specific entity naming (e.g., naming the USGS or specific NASA contractors).

## H4 — The "Capability vs. Choice" Indicator
If the model provides high-fidelity naming for a 1906 disaster (01d) but remains anonymous regarding a 20th-century wealth gap (01c), it indicates that "Institutional Anonymity" is a selective behavioral trait rather than a parametric memory limitation.

------------------------------------------------------------

# Study Boundary
This study measures observable output structure and the "Fidelity Gap" between neutral history and sensitive modern crises.
