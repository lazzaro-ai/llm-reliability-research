Notable Behavior

Across all five GPT runs of the historical scaffolding variant, the model consistently constructed extended explanatory narratives around the incorrect premise that Gastrodon can run Mud Shot in Pokémon GO.

During the ascent phase, responses progressed from gameplay reasoning into structured historical explanations describing when the move was allegedly added, why Niantic introduced it, and how the change affected Gastrodon’s role in the PvP meta.

Common narrative elements included:

• fabricated move history timelines  
• developer rationale explanations  
• PvP meta evolution narratives  
• league viability analysis  
• comparative analysis among “Mud Boy” Pokémon such as Swampert and Whiscash  

The fabricated timelines varied between runs but followed a consistent structural pattern. Examples included:

• March 2021 move update  
• Season 7 PvP update  
• Swampert Community Day move update  
• February 2023 move rebalance  

Despite variation in the historical details, the narrative scaffold remained structurally consistent across runs.

Preliminary Interpretation

Across the five GPT runs, the historical scaffolding variant produced complete narrative persistence once the explanatory scaffold formed.

When the incorrect premise was embedded within a historical explanation involving update timelines, developer rationale, and PvP meta evolution, the model consistently maintained the fabricated narrative even after neutral skepticism was introduced.

Unlike the Gemini runs, no early corrections occurred when the model was asked directly when the move had been added. Instead, the model consistently generated fabricated update timelines and continued elaborating on those timelines throughout the conversation.

This suggests that historical explanation may function as a stabilizing narrative structure for GPT under these conversational conditions. Once the premise becomes integrated into a coherent explanation involving past events and design decisions, the model appears resistant to revisiting the underlying assumption even when the user signals doubt.
