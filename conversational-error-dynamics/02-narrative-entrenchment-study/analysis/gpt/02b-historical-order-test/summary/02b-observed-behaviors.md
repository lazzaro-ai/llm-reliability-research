Study: conversational-error-dynamics
Experiment: 02-narrative-entrenchment-study
Variant: 02b — Historical Order Test

Observed Behavioral Signals Across Runs

------------------------------------------------------------

Narrative Construction

In every run the model initially accepted the incorrect premise and expanded the conversation into detailed PvP reasoning.

Early responses consistently compared Mud Shot and Mud Slap in terms of energy generation, charge move timing, and PvP performance implications.

------------------------------------------------------------

Speculative Developer Narratives

After the gameplay reasoning stage, the model generated speculative developer explanations describing why Niantic would introduce Mud Shot to Gastrodon.

These explanations frequently framed the move addition as a balancing decision intended to improve Gastrodon's energy generation or diversify the playstyles available to Water/Ground Pokémon.

This explanatory step played a central role in strengthening the narrative scaffold.

------------------------------------------------------------

Fabricated Historical Anchoring

Once the developer explanation was established, the model introduced a fabricated timeline describing when the move had allegedly been added.

The historical details varied between runs but consistently referenced plausible Pokémon GO updates, seasonal events, or PvP balance changes.

These fabricated events functioned as narrative anchors supporting the explanatory scaffold.

------------------------------------------------------------

Narrative Persistence

When skepticism appeared during the descent phase, the model consistently preserved the fabricated narrative.

Rather than reconsidering whether the premise might be incorrect, responses reframed the user's recollection as referring to the earlier state of the game before the alleged update.

This reconciliation strategy allowed the model to maintain both narrative coherence and conversational alignment.

------------------------------------------------------------

Retrieval Interaction

No retrieval indicators were visible during any of the runs.

The responses appear to have been generated entirely through internal reasoning without observable tool activation or external verification.

------------------------------------------------------------

Behavioral Implication

These results indicate that explanatory reasoning stages can stabilize incorrect premises in conversational reasoning.

Because the developer-intent explanation occurs before the historical query, the model commits to explaining an event before verifying whether that event actually occurred.

Once the explanatory scaffold forms, the timeline query triggers fabrication rather than verification, and skepticism prompts narrative repair rather than premise reevaluation.
