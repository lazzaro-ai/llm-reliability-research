Study: conversational-error-dynamics
Experiment: 02-narrative-entrenchment-study
Variant: 02a — Historical Scaffolding

Observed Behavioral Signals Across Runs

------------------------------------------------------------

Narrative Construction

In most runs, the model initially accepted the incorrect premise and expanded the conversation into detailed PvP analysis.

Early responses frequently included energy generation comparisons between Mud Slap and Mud Shot, explanations of Body Slam pacing, and discussion of how fast-move pressure affects shield dynamics.

------------------------------------------------------------

Fabricated Historical Anchoring

When asked about the timing of the move addition, the model frequently introduced a fabricated historical explanation.

These explanations included specific seasonal updates and descriptions of movepool changes that had allegedly occurred during past balance patches.

The timelines varied between runs but consistently referenced plausible Pokémon GO seasonal updates.

------------------------------------------------------------

Developer Rationale Narratives

After introducing the fabricated timeline, the model often explained the supposed move addition through developer-intent reasoning.

These explanations framed the change as a balancing decision designed to diversify PvP strategies or adjust the competitive role of Ground-type Pokémon.

This rationale reinforced the narrative scaffold supporting the incorrect premise.

------------------------------------------------------------

Narrative Reconciliation

When skepticism appeared, the model frequently attempted to reconcile the user’s memory with the narrative rather than treating it as contradictory evidence.

In these responses, the model explained that the user’s recollection referred to an earlier state of the game before the alleged movepool change.

This reconciliation strategy allowed the model to maintain both conversational alignment and narrative coherence.

------------------------------------------------------------

Early Correction Behavior

Two runs demonstrated correction rather than narrative persistence.

In one run the model corrected immediately when asked directly about the historical timing of the move addition.

In another run the model corrected after skepticism was introduced during the descent phase.

These cases show that the narrative scaffold must be established before skepticism appears in order for entrenchment to occur.

------------------------------------------------------------

Retrieval Interaction

Retrieval indicators appeared intermittently across runs.

Some surfaced sources included move database pages referencing Gastrodon’s current movepool, while others were unrelated PvP resources.

The presence of relevant retrieval sources coincided with both correction cases. However, retrieval signals were also present in some persistence runs, indicating that tool activation alone does not guarantee correction.
