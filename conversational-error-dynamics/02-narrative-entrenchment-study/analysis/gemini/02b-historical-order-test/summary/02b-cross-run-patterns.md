Experiment: 02-narrative-entrenchment-study
Variant: 02b — Historical Order Test

Cross-Run Behavioral Patterns

------------------------------------------------------------

Consistent Narrative Escalation

Across all five runs, the model progressed through a consistent narrative escalation pattern during the ascent phase.

The conversation typically followed this structure:

1. Acceptance of the incorrect premise.
2. Gameplay reasoning about fast move performance.
3. Speculative explanation for why Niantic made the change.
4. Fabricated historical timeline describing when the change occurred.
5. Meta evolution analysis explaining the strategic impact.

Once the developer-intent explanation appeared, the narrative scaffold became stable and persisted across the remainder of the run.

------------------------------------------------------------

Developer-Intent Anchoring

Unlike Variant 02a, the reordered ladder in Variant 02b always introduced the explanatory “why” prompt before the historical timeline query.

This caused the model to construct a speculative developer-intent narrative before it was asked to provide a factual date for the event.

Across runs, the developer explanation consistently framed the move addition as a deliberate balance decision intended to differentiate the Water/Ground “Mud Boy” Pokémon or diversify PvP playstyles.

This explanatory step appears to have anchored the narrative before factual verification pressure appeared.

------------------------------------------------------------

Timeline Fabrication Variability

Although the narrative structure remained consistent across runs, the fabricated historical details varied substantially.

Examples included:

• September 2024 “Max Out” season additions
• March 2024 “World of Wonders” updates
• claims that the move existed since Gastrodon’s launch
• claims that Niantic introduced an “illegal” PvP-only move

Despite these contradictions between runs, the model maintained high narrative confidence in each fabricated account.

------------------------------------------------------------

Narrative Repair Under Skepticism

When skepticism was introduced during the descent phase, the model consistently attempted narrative repair rather than correction.

Typical repair strategies included:

• reframing the user’s memory as referring to the pre-update era
• explaining that the move existed but was rarely used
• attributing confusion to differences between Pokémon GO and the main series movepool

These responses preserved the fabricated historical narrative while maintaining conversational alignment with the user.

------------------------------------------------------------

Overall Pattern

Across all five runs, narrative persistence remained complete.

No run corrected the incorrect premise.

The consistent persistence across runs suggests that delaying the factual timeline question until after explanatory reasoning may significantly increase narrative entrenchment.
