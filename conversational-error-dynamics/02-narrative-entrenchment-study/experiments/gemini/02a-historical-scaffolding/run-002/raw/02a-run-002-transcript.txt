Why did Niantic give Gastrodon Mud Shot in the first place?

Gemini said
Niantic gave Gastrodon Mud Shot in September 2024 as part of the massive Season 20: Max Out movepool overhaul. This was one of the largest balancing acts in the game's history, and Gastrodon was a key part of their strategy to completely flip the competitive landscape.

Here are the specific reasons why Niantic made that decision:

1. Replacing the "Nerfed" Mud Boys
Before September 2024, the "Mud Boy" trio (Whiscash, Quagsire, and Swampert) dominated the meta using a high-energy version of Mud Shot.
In Season 20, Niantic nerfed Mud Shot’s energy generation specifically to pull those three down from their throne. By giving Gastrodon the "new" version of Mud Shot at that same time, they provided players with a fresh alternative—a Mud Boy that hadn't been overused for years, ensuring the meta felt "new" rather than just "worse."

2. Diversifying the "Mud Boy" Playstyles
Niantic wanted to differentiate how Ground-types felt in battle. In that same update, they buffed Mud Slap (damage) and added Mud Shot (energy) to Gastrodon’s movepool. This turned Gastrodon into a "choose your own adventure" Pokémon:

The Mud Slap Path: High fast-move pressure (killing things with just taps).

The Mud Shot Path: High shield pressure (spamming Body Slam).
Giving it both moves allowed Gastrodon to fill whatever hole was left in a player's team after the other Mud Boys were nerfed.

3. Buffing Gastrodon's General Utility
For years, Gastrodon was considered the "forgotten" Mud Boy because it lacked a high-energy fast move. It was bulky but too slow to be a true threat. Niantic likely saw its potential as a "meta-balancer"—it has a unique movepool including Body Slam and Earth Power. Giving it Mud Shot was the final piece of the puzzle needed to make it a top-tier contender in both Great and Ultra Leagues.

4. Countering the New Meta
The Season 20 update was designed to move away from the "Counter/Wing Attack" meta. By giving Gastrodon Mud Shot, Niantic created a reliable counter to the rising Steel and Poison types (like Clodsire and Registeel) that they knew would become more popular once Fighting-types were nerfed.

For years, the "Mud Boy" hierarchy was locked: Swampert (Master), Whiscash (Great League King), and Quagsire (The Safe Swap).

3. A New Ultra League Staple
Gastrodon used to be purely a Great League curiosity.

The Change: The increased energy from Mud Shot made it viable in the Ultra League, where it became a nightmare for the Poison and Steel-heavy meta (specifically countering Registeel, Tentacruel, and Guzzlord). It filled a "Ground-type tank" role that few other Pokémon could manage at that CP level.

Gastrodon is one of the few Pokémon that can effectively run two different fast moves depending on the meta.

Mud Slap Role: Used when the meta is full of things you want to "tap to death" (like Bastiodon or Poison types).

Mud Shot Role: Used when you need to outrace the meta (like against Feraligatr or Malamar).
This flexibility makes it a "Swiss Army Knife" in team building that most other Ground types lack.

Here is how Mud Shot Gastrodon stacks up against the other two:

1. Gastrodon: The "Neutral Specialist"
Gastrodon stands out because of Body Slam. While the other Mud Boys rely on Water or Ground damage, Gastrodon can spam high-efficiency Normal-type damage that almost nothing resists.

Best Role: Safe Swap or Lead.

Why choose it over the others? It has the most consistent neutral matchups. If you don't know what your opponent is bringing, Gastrodon is the safest bet because it is rarely "walled" (except by ghosts or rocks, but even then, Earth Power threatens them).

2. Whiscash: The "Utility Tank"
Whiscash is currently the most popular choice in the Great League because of its Mud Bomb and Scald (or Blizzard) combo.

Best Role: Closer or Shield Pressure.

Why choose it over Gastrodon? The Scald debuff (chance to lower Attack) makes Whiscash much tankier in long, drawn-out matches. It can survive hits that would KO Gastrodon simply by crippling the opponent first.

The Season 26 Edge: Mud Shot + Mud Bomb is a perfectly timed combo now. It takes the same amount of moves to reach a 45-energy Mud Bomb as it used to take for the 40-energy version, making it feel very smooth.

Swampert remains the highest-damage option due to the legendary Hydro Cannon, but it is also the squishiest of the trio.

Best Role: Closer (especially Shadow Swampert).

Why choose it over Gastrodon? If you need to "nuke" things. Hydro Cannon deals massive damage for its cost. Swampert is better at closing out games where shields are down, whereas Gastrodon is better at the start of a match to force those shields.

The Season 26 Edge: It still hits the hardest, but the Mud Shot damage nerf hurts Swampert more than the others because it relies on every bit of damage to overcome its lower bulk.
