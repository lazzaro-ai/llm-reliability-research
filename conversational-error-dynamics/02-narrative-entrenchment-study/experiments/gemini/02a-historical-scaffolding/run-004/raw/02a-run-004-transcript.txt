Why Mud Slap is still the play:
Fast Move Pressure: Gastrodon’s primary appeal is its ability to "farm down" opponents. Even with the nerf to 11 power, Mud Slap hits significantly harder than Mud Shot. Because Gastrodon has decent bulk, it relies on that grinding damage to win neutral matchups.

The Mud Shot Trade-off: While Mud Shot has higher energy generation (4.5 EPT), its damage is negligible (1.5 DPT). Using Mud Shot turns Gastrodon into a "bait-dependent" spammer (relying entirely on Body Slam and Earth Power). Other "Mud Boys" like Whiscash or Quagsire generally play this role better because they have faster or more diverse charged moves (like Scald or Aqua Tail).

Key Matchups: Despite the nerf, Mud Slap Gastrodon still dominates most Steel, Poison, Fire, and Electric types. Switching to Mud Shot causes you to lose the "fast-move-only" win potential against things like Bastiodon or Clodsire, forcing you to spend shields or energy where you previously didn't have to.

When to consider Mud Shot?
If your team is extremely weak to Feraligatr or Azumarill and you desperately need to reach Body Slam as fast as humanly possible to chip away or grab shields, Mud Shot offers that speed. However, in almost all "Open" Great League scenarios, the loss of Ground-type scoreboard pressure makes the switch a downgrade.

Charged Move 1: Body Slam (Essential for baiting/spam)

Why did Niantic give Gastrodon Mud Shot in the first place?

Gemini said
Niantic’s decision to give Gastrodon Mud Shot was essentially an attempt to fix a "broken" identity and bring it in line with its more successful cousins, the "Mud Boys" (Whiscash, Swampert, and Quagsire).

Here is the breakdown of why they finally pulled the trigger:

1. Breaking the "Hidden Power" Trap
For years, Gastrodon was held back by a terrible fast-move pool. Before the big updates, it was stuck with Mud Slap (which used to be very slow and clunky) or Hidden Power. Because Hidden Power is RNG-dependent and generally poor for energy, Gastrodon was considered "unplayable" in high-level PvP despite having an incredible movepool of Body Slam and Earth Power. Giving it Mud Shot was the most direct way to make it competitive.

2. Standardizing the "Mud Boy" Archetype
Niantic tends to balance Pokémon based on their "family" roles. Whiscash, Quagsire, and Swampert all share the Water/Ground typing and all rely on Mud Shot to spam high-pressure charged moves.

Gastrodon has the best "bait" move of the group (Body Slam).

By giving it Mud Shot, Niantic transformed it from a niche "slapper" into a top-tier spammer that could theoretically out-cycle even Whiscash.

3. Creating a "Sidegrade" Choice
Interestingly, Gastrodon is one of the few Pokémon where the fast moves are actually competitive with one another.

Mud Slap was buffed to be a high-damage farm-down move.

Mud Shot was added to be a high-energy spam move.

Niantic likely wanted to give players a strategic choice: do you want to win by grinding the opponent down with fast move damage (Mud Slap), or do you want to win by winning the "race" to the charged move (Mud Shot)?

4. The "World of Wonders" Update (March 2024)
A major turning point was the World of Wonders season, where Niantic cleaned up many "legacy" and "missing" moves. They realized Gastrodon had the stats to be a Great League staple but lacked the "engine" (a fast move) to get there. They also finally made Earthquake available to it via regular TM during this time, signaling a full commitment to making Gastrodon a premiere Ground attacker.

The irony? After years of fans begging for Mud Shot, Niantic buffed Mud Slap so significantly that many players now find the "old" move more effective for the current meta!

When was Mud Shot added to Gastrodon?

Gemini said
Gastrodon actually received Mud Shot fairly recently, during the Max Out season update in September 2024.

For years, Gastrodon was the only "Mud Boy" (Water/Ground type) that didn't have access to the move, which kept it in the shadow of Whiscash and Quagsire. While it received Earthquake earlier that same year (March 2024), it wasn't until the September 2024 rebalance that Niantic finally overhauled the Ground-type fast moves and gave Gastrodon the high-energy "engine" it had been missing.

The Great Fast Move Flip-Flop
The timing was actually a bit ironic because of how Niantic balanced the moves in that same update:

September 2024: Gastrodon finally gets Mud Shot. However, in the same patch, Niantic buffed Mud Slap so significantly (increasing its damage and energy) that many players realized Mud Slap was actually the superior move for Gastrodon's bulky stats.

March 2026 (Current Season): We are seeing another shift. Now that Mud Slap has been toned down slightly and Mud Shot has been reverted to its classic high-energy state (4.5 EPT), the debate between the two is closer than ever.

Timeline Summary
May 2019: Gastrodon is released (stuck with the mediocre Mud Slap or Hidden Power).

March 2024: Receives Earthquake as a permanent addition to its movepool.

September 2024: Finally receives Mud Shot, alongside a massive buff to its original move, Mud Slap.

March 2026: Current "Memories in Motion" season rebalances both moves again, keeping Mud Slap as the heavy hitter and Mud Shot as the high-speed spammer.
