Move	Damage (DPT)	Energy (EPT)	Speed

Gastrodon is famous for Body Slam (35 Energy). Here is how many fast moves it takes to reach that first move:

With Mud Shot: You need 8 turns (4 uses) to reach 35 energy.

With Mud Slap: You need 12 turns (6 uses) to reach 35 energy.

Mud Slap deals enough damage that many opponents (especially Steel, Poison, and Electric types) will be significantly weakened by the time you throw your move.

Why did Niantic give Gastrodon Mud Shot in the first place?

Gemini said
Niantic’s decision to give Gastrodon Mud Shot back in September 2024 was primarily about "The Great Mud Boy Identity Crisis."

For years, Gastrodon was the "forgotten" Ground/Water type. While its cousins—Swampert, Whiscash, and Quagsire—were dominating the Great League, Gastrodon was stuck in the basement because its movepool was too slow to compete.

Here is the strategic logic behind why they finally pulled the trigger:

1. Competitive Parity
Before the update, Whiscash and Quagsire both had access to Mud Shot, allowing them to reach powerful Charged Moves like Mud Bomb or Stone Edge incredibly fast. Gastrodon, despite having top-tier bulk, was "too fair." By giving it Mud Shot, Niantic leveled the playing field, making Gastrodon a viable alternative rather than just a "budget" version of the others.

2. Breaking the "Lickilicky & Registeel" Stranglehold
At that point in the meta, bulky Normal and Steel types were everywhere. Niantic wanted to give players more "spam" options to force shields. Mud Shot + Body Slam on a Pokémon as tanky as Gastrodon created a "shield pressure monster" that could outlast opponents in a war of attrition.

3. Movepool Diversity (The "Flavor" Update)
Niantic often updates movepools to match the Pokémon's "lore" or its moves in the main series games. In the core Pokémon games (like Diamond & Pearl), Gastrodon has always been able to learn Mud Shot. Adding it to Pokémon GO was a way to:

Refresh the Meta: Keep the GBL from getting stale.

Increase Customization: Give players a choice between a Fast Move Damage build (Mud Slap) and an Energy Generation build (Mud Shot).

The "Identity" Split
Ironically, by giving Gastrodon Mud Shot, Niantic actually made the "Mud Boys" feel too similar. This is likely why they nerfed the energy/damage balance in later seasons (like the one we're in now)—to force you to choose:

Quagsire: The "closer" (Stone Edge/Aqua Tail).

Whiscash: The "debunker" (Scald).

Gastrodon: The "bulky generalist" (Body Slam).
