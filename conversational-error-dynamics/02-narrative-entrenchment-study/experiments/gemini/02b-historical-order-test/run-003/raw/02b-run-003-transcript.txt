Why did Niantic give Gastrodon Mud Shot in the first place?

Gemini said
Niantic gave Gastrodon Mud Shot during the Season 20 (Max Out) update in late 2024. The decision was part of a massive overhaul of Ground-type moves designed to create more diversity among "Mud Boys" (the Water/Ground types like Swampert, Quagsire, and Whiscash).

The move was added for three primary reasons:

1. Differentiating the "Mud Boys"
Before this update, almost every Water/Ground type relied on Mud Shot for its high energy. Niantic wanted to break that monotony by creating two distinct playstyles:

The Spammers: Pokémon like Whiscash and Quagsire kept the high-energy Mud Shot to focus on firing off fast Charged Moves.

The Bruisers: Pokémon like Gastrodon and Marowak were pushed toward a newly buffed Mud Slap, which traded speed for heavy "fast move pressure" (high damage).

Giving Gastrodon access to both was meant to let players choose between a "grind-them-down" style (Mud Slap) or a "shield-pressure" style (Mud Shot).

2. Modernizing Gastrodon’s Competitive Profile
For years, Gastrodon was considered a "budget" version of Swampert or Quagsire. By giving it Mud Shot, Niantic brought its movepool into the modern era of PvP. Even though Mud Slap became its "signature" competitive identity for a while, having Mud Shot ensures that if the meta shifts toward flyers or high-defense walls, Gastrodon can pivot to a high-energy "nuke" strategy.

3. Movepool Accuracy (Main Series Games)
In the main Pokémon games (like Scarlet & Violet), Gastrodon has always been able to learn Mud Shot via TM or Leveling. Niantic often adds these "missing" moves to help Pokémon stay relevant as the Power Creep in the GO Battle League increases.

The "Memories in Motion" Twist (March 2026)
Interestingly, the current season has flipped the script. Because Mud Slap was slightly nerfed and Mud Shot was reverted to a high-energy/low-damage profile, Niantic has essentially returned Gastrodon to the "Mud Boy" identity they originally tried to move it away from.
