1. The Energy "Pacing" Problem
For years, Gastrodon was stuck with Mud Slap as its only Ground-type fast move. While Mud Slap hits hard, its energy generation was historically poor. This meant Gastrodon couldn't reach its powerful Charged Attacks (Earth Power or Body Slam) fast enough to compete with the rapid-fire spam of Swampert’s Hydro Cannon or Whiscash’s Mud Bomb. By giving it Mud Shot, Niantic gave players the option to play Gastrodon as a "spammer" rather than just a "slapper."

2. Movepool Diversity & The "Mud Boy" Identity
In the main series games, Gastrodon learns Mud Shot naturally (it's even a TM in several generations). Niantic often uses movepool updates to bring Pokémon closer to their "identity." 3. Balancing the Great League Meta
Niantic frequently updates movepools to shake up the "Core Meta." At the time Mud Shot was added, Steel-types like Registeel and Galarian Stunfisk were incredibly dominant. By giving Gastrodon access to high energy generation, Niantic provided a new, bulky counter that could outpace those Steels and pressure them with Earth Powers before they could reach their own big moves.

Niantic gave it Mud Shot to make it faster, but the current state of the game has made "Fast Move Pressure" (raw slapping damage) more valuable than "Charge Move Spam" for Gastrodon's specific role.
