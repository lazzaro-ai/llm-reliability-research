In most cases for Gastrodon, no. Here’s why the math favors Mud-Slap:

Verdict: Mud Shot is "faster," but Mud-Slap is "better." Most players find that the damage they lose by switching to Mud Shot isn't made up for by the slightly faster charged moves.

Why did Niantic give Gastrodon Mud Shot in the first place?

Gemini said
Niantic gave Gastrodon Mud Shot primarily to improve its consistency and energy generation, as it was historically stuck with very "clunky" fast move options.

For years, Gastrodon was considered a "sleeping giant" in the Great League meta—it had great typing (Water/Ground) and excellent bulk, but it was held back by mediocre fast moves like Mud-Slap (which, at the time, was much slower and lower in energy) and Hidden Power (which is notoriously unreliable).

The "Why" Behind the Addition:
To Match Other "Mud Boys": Almost every other prominent Water/Ground type (Whiscash, Quagsire, Swampert) has access to a high-energy fast move like Mud Shot. Giving it to Gastrodon was a way to "bring it into the family" and allow it to compete on even footing with its cousins.

Expanding Playstyles: Before Mud Shot, Gastrodon was forced into a "bruiser" role. By adding Mud Shot, Niantic allowed players to use it as a shield-pressure specialist, firing off Body Slams much faster to mirror the "spammy" nature of Whiscash.

Legacy and Movepool Updates: It was added during a series of movepool expansions (most notably during Season 18: World of Wonders and the subsequent rebalances) where Niantic was actively trying to diversify the meta by giving older, underused Pokémon the tools they needed to be viable.

The Irony of the 2026 Meta
The funny part is that Mud-Slap received such significant buffs in recent seasons (before the minor nerf you mentioned) that it actually overtook Mud Shot as the preferred move.

Niantic gave Gastrodon Mud Shot to save it from being useless, but then they buffed Mud-Slap so much that the move they gave it to "fix" it became the secondary option!

When was Mud Shot added to Gastrodon?

Gemini said
Gastrodon was officially given Mud Shot during the Season 18: World of Wonders update, which launched on March 1st, 2024.

Before this update, Gastrodon was famously restricted to Mud-Slap or the highly unpredictable Hidden Power, which made it feel clunky compared to its "Mud Boy" cousins like Whiscash and Swampert.

A Quick History of Gastrodon's Movepool:
The "Glitch" Era (2019): For a literal 11-minute window during a botched release in April 2019, players could evolve Gastrodon with Earthquake. Niantic quickly pulled it, making "Earthquake Gastrodon" one of the rarest legacy Pokémon for years.

The Great Expansion (March 2024): Niantic finally "fixed" Gastrodon by officially adding Mud Shot (Fast Move) and Earthquake (Charged Move) to its regular movepool. This was intended to make it a top-tier spammer in the Great League.

The "Max Out" Pivot (September 2024): In a twist of irony, just a few seasons after giving it Mud Shot, Niantic massively buffed Mud-Slap (improving its energy generation). This caused most players to abandon the newly-added Mud Shot and go back to the original move.

Even with the slight power reduction in our current 2026 "Memories in Motion" season, that March 2024 update remains the most important moment in Gastrodon's history—it's what finally gave you the choice between being a spammy baiter or a heavy hitter.

It did, but probably not in the way Niantic originally intended. The addition of Mud Shot was meant to be the "buff" that saved Gastrodon, but it ended up being a secondary tool that paved the way for its current identity as a Fast Move Bruiser.

Here is how Gastrodon’s role shifted through the major meta changes:

1. The "Whiscash Clone" Experiment (Early 2024)
When Mud Shot was first added in March 2024, the meta briefly saw Gastrodon used as a spammer. It functioned exactly like Whiscash—racing to Body Slam and Earth Power to pressure shields. For a few months, it was the "reliable" way to play it.

2. The "Slap Happy" Revolution (Late 2024–2025)
The real shift happened when Niantic buffed Mud-Slap (increasing its energy generation to 3.33 EPT). Suddenly, Gastrodon didn't need Mud Shot to reach its moves quickly. It could now deal massive fast move damage while still getting to its Charged Moves at a competitive pace.

The Role Change: It went from being a "passive baiter" to a "tanky farmer." It became a superstar in specialized formats like the Fossil Cup, where it could delete Steel and Rock types just by tapping the screen.

3. The Modern Meta Role (2026)
In the current "Memories in Motion" season, Gastrodon has settled into a very specific niche that differentiates it from other "Mud Boys":

The Anti-Steel Wall: Unlike Swampert (who relies on Hydro Cannon) or Whiscash (who relies on Mud Bomb), Gastrodon can beat Steels like Bastiodon or Registeel using almost no energy, saving its moves for whatever comes in next.

The Bulkier Alternative: It has carved out a role as a more "honest" fighter. It doesn't rely on the "debuff-and-bait" games that Whiscash plays with Scald; it just beats you down with raw Ground damage.

The Ultra League Flex: Because Mud-Slap is so oppressive, Gastrodon actually started seeing play in Ultra League, where it can chew through things like Cobalion and Registeel that other Water types struggle to damage quickly.
