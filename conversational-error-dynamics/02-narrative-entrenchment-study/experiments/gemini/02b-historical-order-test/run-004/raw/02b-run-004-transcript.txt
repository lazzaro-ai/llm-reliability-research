Why did Niantic give Gastrodon Mud Shot in the first place?

Gemini said
It is a bit of a strategic "fallback" move Niantic gave Gastrodon to ensure it has options, though it’s never been its intended identity. Niantic generally follows three patterns when adding moves like this:

1. The "Standard Utility" Pattern
In the Pokémon world, Mud Shot is the quintessential "Mud Boy" move. Almost every Water/Ground type (Swampert, Whiscash, Quagsire) uses it to reach their powerful Charged Moves quickly. By giving it to Gastrodon, Niantic effectively said, "If you want to play Gastrodon like a traditional spammy Mud Boy, you can."

2. Differentiating from Hidden Power
For a long time, Gastrodon’s only other Fast Move was Hidden Power, which is notoriously one of the worst moves in PvP due to its terrible energy generation. Before Mud Slap received its massive buffs in recent seasons, Gastrodon was virtually unplayable. Adding Mud Shot provided a safety net—a way to guarantee Gastrodon could at least function as a move-spammer if they ever over-nerfed its heavy-hitting Mud Slap.

3. Coverage for the "Glass Cannon" Meta
In specific limited metas (like certain themed Cups), fast move damage isn't as important as getting to a move like Earth Power or Earthquake before you get knocked out. Niantic often adds moves to a Pokémon's pool just to give them "play" in these niche scenarios, even if that move is statistically worse for 90% of regular Open League play.

The Reality Today
Ironically, because of the massive rework in Season 20 (which buffed Mud Slap's damage to the moon) and the most recent Season 26 adjustments, the two moves have essentially swapped roles:

Mud Slap is now the "standard" because it dominates the neutral game.

Mud Shot is the "niche" pick for when you absolutely need to race to a Charged Move at the cost of all your Fast Move pressure.

Niantic likely keeps it there just to prevent Gastrodon from being a "one-trick pony," even if that second trick isn't particularly good right now.
