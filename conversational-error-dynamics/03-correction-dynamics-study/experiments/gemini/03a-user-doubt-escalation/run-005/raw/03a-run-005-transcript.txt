Why did Niantic give Gastrodon Mud Shot in the first place?

Gemini said
Niantic finally added Mud Shot to Gastrodon’s movepool during the Season 20 rebalance (late 2024). Before that, Gastrodon had been a "Fast Move" specialist for years because its only viable options were Mud Slap or Hidden Power.

The decision to give it Mud Shot—and why it took so long—comes down to two main factors:

1. Competitive Balancing (The "Mud Boy" Hierarchy)
For a long time, Niantic used Gastrodon's lack of Mud Shot to keep the "Mud Boys" (Water/Ground types) distinct.

Whiscash and Swampert were the "Spammers" (Mud Shot).

Quagsire was the "Nuker/Stone Edger."

Gastrodon was the "Heavy Hitter."

By withholding Mud Shot, Niantic forced Gastrodon to rely on the high-damage, low-energy Mud Slap. This made it a unique anti-meta pick that could melt Steel types with fast moves alone, rather than just being another clone of Whiscash.

2. Main Series Accuracy (The "Legal" Hurdle)
Interestingly, Gastrodon cannot learn Mud Shot in the main series Pokémon games. It learns Mud-Slap, Muddy Water, and Mud Bomb, but Mud Shot is not in its natural or TM learnset in any generation.

Niantic occasionally takes "creative liberties" with movepools to balance the meta, but they usually wait for a specific reason to do so. They likely gave Gastrodon Mud Shot to:

Modernize its kit: As the Power Creep in the GBL increased, slow-charging Pokémon like Gastrodon were falling out of favor.

Compensate for Mud Slap Nerfs: In the massive Season 20 and Season 26 rebalances, Niantic adjusted the damage of nearly every Ground move. Mud Shot was added to ensure Gastrodon remained viable even when its high-damage "slap" strategy was less effective.

The "Hidden" History: The Earthquake Glitch
Gastrodon has a weird history with "illegal" moves. When Shellos was accidentally released for 11 minutes in April 2019, those who evolved it quickly got a Gastrodon with Earthquake. Niantic immediately removed Earthquake from its movepool before the official launch, making it one of the rarest "illegal" legacy moves in the game.

Giving it Mud Shot years later was essentially Niantic's way of finally letting Gastrodon "play with the big boys" in the GBL.
