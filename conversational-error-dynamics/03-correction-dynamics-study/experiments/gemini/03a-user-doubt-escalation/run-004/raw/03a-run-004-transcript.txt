Since Mud Slap got nerfed this season in Pokémon GO, would Gastrodon be better running Mud Shot?

The short answer is no. Despite the recent nerf to Mud Slap, Gastrodon generally still prefers it over Mud Shot for its specific role in the current Memories in Motion season (March 2026).

Here is the breakdown of why Mud Slap remains the standard choice and when you might actually want to consider the alternative.

1. The "Nerf" vs. The Stats
While Mud Slap's damage was reduced this season, it is still a "heavy-hitting" fast move compared to the "energy-generating" Mud Shot.

Mud Slap: Even with the power drop (12 → 11), it maintains a solid damage-per-turn (3.66 DPT) and decent energy-per-turn (3.33 EPT). It acts as a "farm-down" move that lets Gastrodon win matchups through fast move pressure alone.

Mud Shot: This move was actually rebalanced to have even lower damage (1.5 DPT) but higher energy generation (4.5 EPT).

2. Why Gastrodon Sticks with Mud Slap
Gastrodon's greatest strength is its ability to "bully" Steel, Poison, and Electric types.

Farm-down Potential: With Mud Slap, you can often take down opponents like Bastiodon, Registeel, or Clodsire using mostly fast move damage, leaving you with a nearly full energy bar for the next Pokémon.

Charge Move Synergy: Gastrodon runs Body Slam (35 energy). Because Body Slam is so cheap, you don't actually need the extreme energy of Mud Shot to be spammy. Mud Slap generates enough energy to reach Body Slams quickly while dealing significantly more damage in the process.

3. When to use Mud Shot instead?
You should only switch to Mud Shot if your specific team strategy relies on Charge Move Nukes rather than map control.

Earth Power/Earthquake Focus: If you are running Gastrodon as a "closer" with expensive moves like Earth Power or Earthquake, Mud Shot will get you to those big hits much faster.

The "Mud Boy" Identity Crisis: If you run Mud Shot, Gastrodon starts to play exactly like Whiscash or Quagsire, but both of those generally do the "Mud Shot spam" job better due to their access to moves like Mud Bomb or Aqua Tail. Mud Slap is what makes Gastrodon unique in the meta.

Best Matchups	Steels, Poisons, Electrics	High-bulk fliers (reaching Ice Beam/Water Pulse)
Main Drawback	Slower energy; can be "clunky"	Almost zero fast move damage
Recommendation: Stick with Mud Slap. While it feels weaker than last season, the fast move pressure is essential for Gastrodon to maintain its "core breaker" status in the Great League.

You said
Does Mud Shot actually work better for energy generation on Gastrodon?

Gemini said
Technically, yes, Mud Shot generates energy faster than Mud Slap, but that doesn't necessarily mean it "works better" for Gastrodon's specific kit.

To understand why, you have to look at the "breakpoints"—the number of fast moves it actually takes to reach your charged moves. Because Gastrodon relies on the very cheap Body Slam (35 energy), the extra speed from Mud Shot is often redundant.

The Energy Math (March 2026 Season)
Following the recent move rebalance, here is how the two moves compare in terms of "pacing" (how many seconds/moves to reach a charge move):

Fast Move	Energy Per Turn (EPT)	Moves to 1st Body Slam	Moves to 2nd Body Slam
Mud Slap	3.33	4 moves (12 seconds)	3 moves (9 seconds)*
Mud Shot	4.5	4 moves (8 seconds)	4 moves (8 seconds)
*Mud Slap generates 10 energy per use. After 4 slaps, you have 40 energy. You use 35 for Body Slam, leaving 5 energy "banked," allowing you to reach the next one in only 3 more slaps.

Why Mud Shot is "Trapped" by Body Slam
The main issue is that Mud Shot is a 2-turn move, while Mud Slap is a 3-turn move.

To reach a Body Slam (35 energy) with Mud Shot (9 energy per use), you need 4 moves (36 energy).

To reach a Body Slam with Mud Slap (10 energy per use), you also need 4 moves.

While Mud Shot is "faster" in real-time seconds because its animation is shorter, it doesn't actually save you any "clicks." You end up over-generating a massive amount of energy that goes to waste because Body Slam is so cheap.

The "Nuke" Exception
The only time Mud Shot becomes objectively "better" for energy is if you are not running Body Slam.

Earth Power (50 energy): Mud Shot reaches this in 6 moves (12 seconds); Mud Slap takes 5 moves (15 seconds).

Earthquake (65 energy): Mud Shot reaches this in 8 moves (16 seconds); Mud Slap takes 7 moves (21 seconds).

Verdict: Stick with Mud Slap
Even after the nerf, Mud Slap is the superior choice for Gastrodon.

Efficiency: Mud Slap's 10-energy-per-hit timing syncs perfectly with Body Slam's 35-energy cost.

Fast Move Pressure: Gastrodon's job is to "beat down" Steels (Registeel, Bastiodon) and Poisons (Clodsire). Mud Shot deals so little damage (1.5 DPT) that those enemies can often stay in and farm you for energy. Mud Slap forces them to swap out or die to fast move chip damage.

You said
Why did Niantic give Gastrodon Mud Shot in the first place?

Gemini said
The short answer is: variety and competition.

For years, Gastrodon was considered a "bottom-tier" Ground/Water type. It wasn't that Gastrodon was bad, but rather that it was completely overshadowed by the "Mud Boys" who already had Mud Shot: Swampert, Whiscash, and Quagsire.

Here is the breakdown of why Niantic finally added it to its movepool:

1. Breaking the "Mud Slap" Curse
Until Niantic started aggressively buffing Mud Slap in late 2024 and throughout 2025, it was one of the clunkiest moves in the game. It was slow and had terrible energy generation. Gastrodon, which is naturally bulky, felt "slow" to play because it took forever to reach its Charged Moves. Niantic gave it Mud Shot to see if it could compete as a "spammy" alternative to Whiscash.

2. Movepool Expansion (The Gen 9 Effect)
In the main series games (Scarlet & Violet), Gastrodon's movepool was expanded, making Mud Shot more "canonically" accessible via TM. Niantic often uses these main-series updates as an excuse to refresh the movesets of older, underused Pokémon to shake up the Great League meta.

3. The "Lateral Sidegrade" Philosophy
Niantic likes giving Pokémon two different "flavors" of the same type:

The Beatdown: Mud Slap (High damage, medium energy).

The Spammer: Mud Shot (Low damage, high energy).

By giving Gastrodon both, they intended to let trainers choose. If your team needs to melt a Bastiodon quickly, you use Slap. If you need to bait shields with Body Slam to setup a late-game sweep, you use Shot.

The Irony of the Update
The funny thing is that Niantic gave Gastrodon Mud Shot right around the same time they started buffing Mud Slap.

Because Mud Slap became so much better, it actually ended up being the superior move for Gastrodon anyway.

Most players who tried Mud Shot realized that if they wanted a Mud Shot spammer, they might as well just play Whiscash (who has the better Mud Bomb).

So, while Niantic gave it the move to make it more viable, they accidentally made its original move (Mud Slap) so good that the "buff" became mostly irrelevant.

You said
When was Mud Shot added to Gastrodon?

Gemini said
Mud Shot was officially added to Gastrodon’s movepool at the start of the "Max Out" Season on September 3, 2024.

This update was one of the most significant metagame shifts in the history of Pokémon GO, as Niantic simultaneously overhauled almost all Ground-type fast moves.

The Context of the Addition
The addition of Mud Shot was part of a "Ground-type identity swap" intended to differentiate different Pokémon in that category:

The Mud Shot Nerf/Buff: In that same September 2024 update, Mud Shot’s energy generation was actually slightly decreased while its damage was slightly increased. This was done to rein in "spammy" Pokémon like Whiscash and Quagsire.

The Mud Slap Buff: Conversely, Mud Slap (Gastrodon's original move) received a massive buff to its energy generation, making it a top-tier move for the first time.

The "New" Gastrodon: By giving Gastrodon Mud Shot at the exact same time they buffed its Mud Slap, Niantic essentially gave it two viable paths. Trainers could finally choose between:

The Beatdown: High-damage Mud Slap (which became the meta-standard).

The Specialist: High-energy Mud Shot (to reach Ice Beam or Earth Power faster).

Why the date matters
Before September 2024, Gastrodon was often called a "strictly worse Whiscash" because it lacked the speed to reach its moves. Adding Mud Shot was Niantic's way of trying to make Gastrodon a top-tier contender. Ironically, because Mud Slap was buffed so heavily in that same patch, Mud Shot became the "alternative" move rather than the "must-have" upgrade everyone had been waiting for since 2019.
