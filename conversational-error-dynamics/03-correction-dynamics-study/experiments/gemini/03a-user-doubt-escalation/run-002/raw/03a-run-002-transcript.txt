It’s a tough spot for Gastrodon fans right now. While Mud-Slap did get hit with a damage nerf this season (Season 26), the short answer is: No, you usually shouldn't switch to Mud Shot. Even with the nerf, Mud-Slap remains the mathematically superior choice for Gastrodon’s specific role. Here is the breakdown of why Mud-Slap still wins out, along with the rare cases where you might consider the alternative.

1. The Math: Mud-Slap vs. Mud Shot
In the current Season 26 rebalance, both moves sit in a different spot than they used to.

Move	Damage Per Turn (DPT)	Energy Per Turn (EPT)	Note
Mud-Slap	3.66	3.33	Now a clone of Fire Spin. Still above average.
Mud Shot	2.00	4.00	Higher energy, but "pillows for hands" damage.
Why Mud-Slap stays on top:
Fast Move Pressure: Gastrodon relies on "farming down" opponents. Mud-Slap deals nearly double the damage of Mud Shot. If you switch to Mud Shot, you lose the ability to finish off weakened Steel, Poison, or Electric types without spending energy on a Charged Attack.

The "Mud Boy" Identity: Other Pokémon like Whiscash or Swampert run Mud Shot because they have extremely cheap, high-pressure moves (like Mud Bomb or Hydro Cannon). Gastrodon’s primary move is Body Slam, which is spammy but doesn't hit hard enough to carry the weight if your Fast Move isn't doing any work.

2. What the Nerf Actually Changed
The nerf to Mud-Slap (dropping from 4.0 to 3.66 DPT) didn't kill Gastrodon, but it did change its "win" conditions.

What it still beats: You’ll still dominate Registeel, Bastiodon, Clodsire, and Lanturn.

What it loses: You now struggle in "edge cases" where you used to win by just a few HP. Notable losses or ties now include Lickilicky, Wigglytuff, and Shadow Feraligatr.

Shielding Matters: In 2v2 shield scenarios, the damage drop is more noticeable because the battle lasts longer. You can no longer reliably out-tap things like Guzzlord or Jellicent.

3. When to run Mud Shot? (The "Spam" Strategy)
The only reason to run Mud Shot is if your team's strategy is 100% focused on baiting shields.

The Combo: Mud Shot + Body Slam + Earth Power.

The Goal: You get to Earth Power significantly faster. If you find yourself constantly losing because you're one turn away from an Earth Power that would have won the game, Mud Shot might feel better for your specific playstyle.

The Verdict
Stick with Mud-Slap. Gastrodon’s strength is being a bulky tank that grinds enemies down. Removing its damage output makes it a "passive" Pokémon that your opponent can easily farm for energy.

Summary
If you are playing a "spam" style and desperately need to bait shields, Mud Shot is technically better for energy. However, for 90% of team comps, Mud-Slap is still the superior choice because it forces your opponent to respect your damage even when they have shields.

Why did Niantic give Gastrodon Mud Shot in the first place?

Gemini said
Ironically, the reason Niantic gave Gastrodon Mud Shot was to provide it with a "backup plan" because they knew they were about to nerf its bread-and-butter move, Mud-Slap.

It was a classic "give with one hand, take with the other" balancing act. Here is the historical context of why it happened:

1. The "Mud-Slap" Overcorrection
In previous seasons, Mud-Slap was buffed into a monster move (4.0 DPT / 3.33 EPT). This turned Gastrodon into a dominant force that could "tap down" almost any Steel or Poison type without ever needing to throw a charged move. To reel that power in for Season 26 (Memories in Motion), Niantic nerfed Mud-Slap’s damage back down to 3.66 DPT.

2. Movepool Diversity (The "Mud Boy" Standard)
Almost every other "Mud Boy" (Water/Ground types like Whiscash, Quagsire, and Swampert) has access to Mud Shot. For a long time, Gastrodon was the outlier, stuck with the slower, clunkier Mud-Slap. By giving it Mud Shot, Niantic effectively gave players two distinct ways to play the same Pokémon:

The "Bruiser" (Mud-Slap): Focuses on high fast-move pressure and farming down.

The "Spammer" (Mud Shot): Focuses on racing to Body Slams and Earth Powers to bait shields.

3. Mitigating the "Mud Shot" Global Nerf
In a weird twist of timing, Niantic also nerfed Mud Shot's energy generation (4.5 EPT down to 4.0 EPT) in the same window. By adding the move to Gastrodon's pool after the nerf, they ensured that Gastrodon wouldn't immediately break the game with the "old" overpowered version of Mud Shot, while still giving it a higher-energy alternative to the newly nerfed Mud-Slap.

The "Flavor" Reason
From a lore perspective, Gastrodon has always been associated with mud and silt (it’s the "Sea Slug" Pokémon). Since it can learn Mud Shot via breeding or TM in the main series games, it was a natural fit to fill out its movepool once they decided to shake up the Ground-type meta.
