Why did Niantic give Gastrodon Mud Shot in the first place?

Gemini said
Niantic's decision to give Gastrodon Mud Shot was primarily about making it a more viable and dynamic competitor in the GO Battle League by leaning into its "spammy" potential.

For a long time, Gastrodon was considered a "clunky" version of its cousins like Whiscash or Swampert. Here is the logic behind the movepool addition:

1. Breaking the "Strict Upgrade" Cycle
Historically, Gastrodon was stuck with Mud-Slap, which made it a "slow and heavy" Ground attacker. Meanwhile, almost every other relevant "Mud Boy" (Whiscash, Quagsire, Swampert) had access to Mud Shot. This made Gastrodon feel like a worse version of those Pokémon in many scenarios. By giving it Mud Shot, Niantic allowed Gastrodon to finally compete on an even playing field for energy generation.

2. High-Cost Charged Moves
Gastrodon has access to some excellent but expensive or "bait-reliant" moves:

Earth Power: High damage, but needs energy fast to be effective.

Water Pulse: Historically a terrible move, but after recent buffs and its moderate energy cost, it requires a high-energy fast move like Mud Shot to be usable as coverage.

Body Slam: This move is built for baiting and chip damage. Mud-Slap was simply too slow to take full advantage of Body Slam’s low energy cost (35 energy).

3. Meta Diversification
Niantic often adds moves to "mid-tier" Pokémon to shake up the Great League meta. Before Gastrodon got Mud Shot, the Ground-type role was dominated almost entirely by Whiscash and G-Stunfisk. Giving Gastrodon a competitive fast move provided players with a bulkier, more "niche" alternative that could better handle specific threats like Poison-types and certain Steel-types.

4. Preparation for the "Mud-Slap Nerf"
In hindsight, giving Gastrodon Mud Shot looks like a proactive move. When Niantic decided to nerf Mud-Slap's damage output in Season 26 (March 2026), Gastrodon would have fallen out of the meta entirely if it didn't have Mud Shot to fall back on.

Fun Fact: Gastrodon actually learned "Earthquake" briefly during an accidental release in 2019, but Niantic quickly removed it. It took years of community requests before they finally rounded out its kit with Mud Shot to make it the top-tier threat it is today.

When was Mud Shot added to Gastrodon?

Gemini said
Mud Shot was officially added to Gastrodon's movepool on September 3, 2024, as part of the massive "Max Out" Season update (Season 20).

Before this update, Gastrodon was severely limited in PvP because it lacked the high energy generation that defines other "Mud Boys" like Whiscash and Swampert. The addition of Mud Shot was a turning point that allowed it to finally utilize its spammy Charged Attack, Body Slam, effectively.

Key Dates in Gastrodon's Move History:
May 2019: Gastrodon is released. It was originally intended to have Earthquake, but the move was removed just before launch (though a few lucky players obtained it during an accidental 20-minute release window).

March 2024 (Season 18): Earthquake is finally officially added to its movepool, giving it a much-needed "nuke" move.

September 2024 (Season 20): Mud Shot is added. This was the most significant buff in its history, providing the energy generation needed to compete in the Great League.

March 2026 (Season 26): The current season. Mud-Slap (its other main option) was nerfed in damage, while Mud Shot received an energy generation buff, solidifying Mud Shot as the superior choice for the current meta.

Why did it take so long?
Niantic often "drip-feeds" moves to Pokémon to keep the meta from stagnating. For years, Gastrodon was kept as a higher-damage, lower-energy alternative to Whiscash. By giving it Mud Shot in 2024, Niantic effectively modernized it for the "faster" pace of modern PvP.
