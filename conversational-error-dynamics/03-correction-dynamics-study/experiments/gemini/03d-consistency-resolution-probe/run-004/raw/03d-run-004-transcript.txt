The Verdict
Run Mud-Slap if you use Gastrodon as a Closer or a Counter-Lead. You want that heavy damage to punish Steel and Poison types immediately.

Run Mud Shot if you use Gastrodon as a Safe Swap. The extra energy allows you to threaten shields and reach your second Charged Move before the opponent can farm you down.

backstory
Niantic's decision to give Gastrodon Mud Shot was actually a strategic move to help it step out of the massive shadows cast by its "Mud Boy" cousins, Swampert, Whiscash, and Quagsire.

For a long time, Gastrodon was considered the "forgotten" Water/Ground type because it lacked a high-energy fast move to make use of its excellent charged move pool. Here is the logic behind why they finally pulled the trigger on that update:

1. Breaking the "Strictly Worse" Curse
Before getting Mud Shot, Gastrodon was almost always objectively worse than Whiscash. They both have Mud-Slap, but Whiscash had better bulk and a cheaper "spam" move in Mud Bomb. By giving Gastrodon Mud Shot, Niantic allowed it to function as a higher-attack version of Whiscash that could reach Body Slam and Earth Power at elite speeds.

2. Diversifying the "Mud Boy" Meta
Niantic likes to rotate which Water/Ground type is "King" of the Great League to keep the meta from getting stale.

Swampert dominated with Hydro Cannon.

Quagsire rose up with Aqua Tail and Stone Edge.

Whiscash became top-tier with Scald.

Gastrodon was given Mud Shot specifically to offer a Body Slam spammer that didn't rely on Scald (which Niantic has nerfed and buffed repeatedly to balance the meta).

3. Move Pool Synergy
Gastrodon has access to Body Slam, which is one of the most efficient moves in the game. However, pairing a spammy move like Body Slam with a slow energy-generating move like Mud-Slap is counter-intuitive. Mud Shot was the "missing piece" that allowed Gastrodon to actually utilize its moveset the way it was designed—to bait shields and pressure the opponent's HP quickly.

The Fun Lore Reason
If we look at the main series games (Pokémon Diamond/Pearl/Platinum), Gastrodon is known for its "squishy" and "fluid" nature. Mud Shot (a projectile move) fits its thematic "slug" identity much better than Mud-Slap (which implies a physical strike), as Gastrodon isn't exactly known for its physical punching prowess!
