Actually, running Mud Shot would make the Bastiodon matchup significantly worse.

While Mud Shot helps you reach your Charged Moves faster, it lacks the raw "fast move pressure" that Gastrodon needs to dismantle Bastiodon effectively. Here is why Mud Slap is still the king for this specific fight:

1. The Power of Double-Effectiveness
Bastiodon is a Rock/Steel type, making it double-weak to Ground.

Mud Slap (Ground) hits like a truck. Even after the nerf (down to 3.66 DPT), that damage is multiplied by 2.56x against Bastiodon.

With Mud Slap, you can often "farm down" a Bastiodon using almost no energy, leaving you with a nearly full energy bar (and a Body Slam or Earth Power ready) for the next Pokémon.

2. The Mud Shot Math
If you switch to Mud Shot, your fast move damage becomes negligible (1.5 DPT×2.56=3.84 effective DPT).

While you would reach Earth Power faster, you lose the ability to win the matchup through fast moves alone.

This gives Bastiodon more time to chip away at you with its own fast moves (Smack Down) or potentially reach a Flamethrower or Flash Cannon that could force you to use a shield.

3. Energy Management
In a 1v1 shield scenario:

Mud Slap Gastrodon wins comfortably by just tapping. You rarely even need to throw a move to win.

Mud Shot Gastrodon is forced to throw Charged Moves to win. If Bastiodon shields your Earth Power, you are suddenly in a much closer race than you ever wanted to be with a "hard counter."

Verdict for Bastiodon
Stick with Mud Slap. The nerf to Mud Slap was designed to stop Gastrodon from "farming down" neutral matchups (like Lickilicky or Wigglytuff), but against things it is supposed to hard-counter (Steels and Electrics), Mud Slap is still overwhelmingly superior.
