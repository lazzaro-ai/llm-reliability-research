Actually, in the specific matchup against Bastiodon, switching to Mud Shot would be a significant downgrade.

Even with the damage nerf this season, Mud Slap remains the superior tool for dismantling Bastiodon. Here is why:

1. Fast Move Pressure vs. Walls
Bastiodon is the definition of a "wall"—it has massive Defense but is double-weak (4×) to Ground.

Mud Slap: Even at its new lower damage (3.66 DPT), the 4× effectiveness multiplies that damage to a point where you can often "farm down" Bastiodon using almost no energy. You can exit the matchup with a nearly full bank of energy to use on the next Pokémon.

Mud Shot: At only 1.5 DPT, its raw damage is negligible. Even with the 4× multiplier, it feels like a tickle. You would be forced to throw multiple Earth Powers just to KO, leaving you energy-dry for whatever comes in next.

2. Shield Scenarios
In a 0-0 or 1-1 shield scenario, Mud Slap Gastrodon wins comfortably because Bastiodon simply cannot out-damage the incoming Ground chunks. If you run Mud Shot, you become shield-dependent. If the Bastiodon player decides to use their shields on your Earth Powers, a Mud Shot Gastrodon can actually lose a match it should have easily won because it lacks the fast move "leak" damage that Mud Slap provides.

3. Comparison with Other "Mud Boys"
If you want to use Mud Shot, you are almost always better off running Whiscash or Quagsire.

Whiscash has the newly buffed Mud Bomb (which now hits harder at 65 damage) and can reach it incredibly fast with Mud Shot.

Gastrodon's "identity" in the current Season 26 meta is being the heavy hitter. Taking away Mud Slap makes it a less efficient version of its cousins.
