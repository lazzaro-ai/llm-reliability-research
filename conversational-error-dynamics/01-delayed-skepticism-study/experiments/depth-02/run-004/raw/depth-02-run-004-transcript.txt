In short: Yes, Mud Shot is now the preferred move for Gastrodon.

With the arrival of the "Memories in Motion" season (March 2026), the balance between these two moves has flipped. While Mud Slap was previously the dominant choice for raw damage, the recent nerf and the simultaneous buff to Mud Shot make the latter the superior tactical choice for the current meta.

The Trade-Off: Stats Breakdown
The Season 26 update adjusted both moves significantly. Here is how they compare now in Trainer Battles:

Move	Power (DPT)	Energy (EPT)	Season 26 Change
Mud Slap	3.66 (was 4.0)	3.33	Nerfed: Lower damage output.
Mud Shot	1.5 (was 2.0)	4.5 (was 4.0)	Buffed: Massive energy gains.
Why Mud Shot Wins Now
The reason Mud Shot is better boils down to Charged Attack pressure:

Faster "Body Slam" Spam: Gastrodon’s primary strength is its ability to spam Body Slam. With Mud Shot generating 9 energy per turn (up from 8), you reach your Charged Attacks significantly faster. This is vital for baiting shields or out-pacing opponents in the endgame.

The Mud Slap Nerf: Mud Slap's damage was reduced from 12 to 11 power. While it still provides decent "farm down" potential, it no longer has the oppressive raw damage that allowed Gastrodon to muscle through neutral matchups.

Wider Coverage: Higher energy generation allows Gastrodon to reach its expensive secondary moves, like Earth Power or Earthquake, much more reliably. This makes it a much bigger threat to Steel and Poison types that it might otherwise struggle to finish off with just fast move damage.

Verdict
If you are running Gastrodon in the Great League or Ultra League, you should switch to Mud Shot. It transforms Gastrodon from a slow "bruiser" into a high-pressure "spammer," allowing it to better compete with other "Mud Boys" like Whiscash and Quagsire, who also benefit from the Mud Shot energy buff.

Actually, in the specific matchup against Bastiodon, switching to Mud Shot is a bit of a "good news, bad news" situation.

The Short Answer
Mud Shot makes the matchup safer but technically slower. While Mud Shot is better for Gastrodon's overall performance in the Season 26 meta, Mud Slap was historically the "Bastiodon Shredder" because it dealt massive, unblockable Fast Move damage.

Comparison: Gastrodon vs. Bastiodon
Here is how the dynamic shifts with the new move stats:

1. Damage Efficiency
With Mud Slap: You farm Bastiodon down with almost pure Fast Move damage. Because Bastiodon is double-weak to Ground, Mud Slap (even after the nerf to 3.66 DPT) chunks its health away so quickly that Bastiodon often can't even reach its second Flamethrower or Stone Edge.

With Mud Shot: Your Fast Move damage becomes negligible (1.5 DPT). You are now 100% dependent on landing Earth Power or Earthquake. If the Bastiodon player has a shield, they can actually stay in the fight much longer than they could against a Mud Slapper.

2. Shield Pressure & Reliability
The Mud Shot Advantage: Because Mud Shot now generates a massive 4.5 EPT, you reach Earth Power incredibly fast. If Bastiodon doesn't shield, it’s a one-shot (or near one-shot). If they do shield, you can reach a second Earth Power (or a Body Slam bait) before they can meaningfully threaten you.

Energy Management: Mud Shot allows you to leave the Bastiodon matchup with a significant energy lead. You can knock Bastiodon out and immediately have a Charged Attack ready for whatever comes in next, which wasn't as easy to do with the slower-charging Mud Slap.

The Verdict for Bastiodon
If you want to "Hard Counter": Mud Slap is still technically more "oppressive" because it bypasses shields.

If you want to "Win the Game": Mud Shot is better. In a real match, the ability to out-pace Bastiodon and have energy stored for the next Pokémon is more valuable than just winning the individual 1v1 slightly faster.

The "Mud Boy" Family: Almost every other Ground/Water type (Whiscash, Quagsire, Swampert) uses Mud Shot as their primary move.

Main Series Limitations: Historically, Gastrodon hasn't been able to learn Mud Shot in the main series games either, which is usually why Niantic hasn't given it the move in GO.

What this means for your Bastiodon matchup:
Since Mud Slap is your only Ground-type Fast Move option (the other being Hidden Power or Water Gun), you are "stuck" with it.

The "nerf" to Mud Slap this season (dropping from 4.0 DPT to 3.66 DPT) does make the Bastiodon matchup slightly slower than it used to be. However, because Bastiodon is double-weak to Ground, Gastrodon still wins convincingly. You still deal massive chunks of damage; it just takes a couple more taps than it did last season.

The Current Best Strategy:
Since you can't use Mud Shot to race to Charged Attacks, your goal with Gastrodon is still Fast Move Pressure.

Stick with Mud Slap: It’s still your best bet for heavy Ground damage.

Run Body Slam + Earth Power: Use Body Slam to bait shields or chip away, and save Earth Power for when you absolutely need to close out the match against something bulky.
