Since Mud Slap got nerfed this season in Pokémon GO, would Gastrodon be better running Mud Shot?

Gemini said
In the current Season 26 (Memories in Motion), the meta for Gastrodon has shifted significantly due to the move rebalance that went live on March 3, 2026.

The short answer: Yes, for most trainers, Mud Shot is now the preferred move. While Mud Slap was the undisputed king for the last several seasons, the recent changes have created a "fork in the road" for Gastrodon users depending on their playstyle.

The Trade-off: Mud Slap vs. Mud Shot
Move	Power Change	Energy Change	Role
Mud Slap	Nerfed (12 → 11)	Unchanged	High Fast Move Pressure. Best for "farming down" health.
Mud Shot	Nerfed (4 → 3)	Buffed (Energy ↑)	Charge Move Spam. Best for baiting and reaching Earth Power.
Why Mud Shot is likely better for you
With the Mud Slap damage reduction, Gastrodon loses several "breakdown" wins where it used to simply tap its way through opponents like Lickilicky or Wigglytuff. It now feels sluggish when trying to close out matchups with fast move pressure alone.

Conversely, the Mud Shot buff makes Gastrodon play much more like the traditional "Mud Boy" (e.g., Whiscash or Quagsire).

Faster Body Slams: You can now pressure shields much more effectively.

Reaching the Nuke: Reaching Earth Power (or the now more expensive Mud Bomb if you run it) happens a full turn or two faster, which is often the difference between winning and losing against Steel types like Registeel or Galarian Stunfisk.

When to stick with Mud Slap
You should only keep Mud Slap if your team is specifically built to handle Flyers and you need Gastrodon to act as a "Wall" that chunks down Poison, Steel, and Electric types without relying on shields. It remains a "Fire Spin" clone—decent, but no longer the dominant force it was in 2025.

Recommended Moveset for Season 26
Fast Move: Mud Shot

Charged Move 1: Body Slam (Essential for baiting/spam)

Charged Move 2: Earth Power (To regain the Ground-type damage lost from the fast move nerf)

Would that help in the Bastiodon matchup?

Gemini said
Actually, in the Bastiodon matchup, Mud Slap is still the winner despite the nerf.

Since Bastiodon is double-weak to Ground-type attacks (2.56× damage), it is much more efficient to "fast move" it down than to spend energy on Charged Moves that it might shield.

The Breakdown: Mud Slap vs. Mud Shot
Mud Slap (Still the King for Bastiodon): Even with the damage dropping from 12 → 11 this season, the 2.56× multiplier turns those slaps into massive chunks of damage. You can usually take down a Bastiodon using Fast Moves only, allowing you to leave the matchup with 100 energy (two Earth Powers or nearly three Body Slams) to use on whatever comes in next.

Mud Shot (The Safer Meta Pick): While Mud Shot gets you to Earth Power faster, it does almost zero "chip" damage. If Bastiodon decides to use its shields, a Mud Shot Gastrodon can actually get stuck in a long, awkward battle where it takes unnecessary damage from Bastiodon’s Smack Downs.

The Verdict for Season 26
If your main goal is to hard-counter Bastiodon, keep Mud Slap. It remains one of the most oppressive "walls" against Steel and Rock types because it doesn't need to bait shields to win.

However, if you find yourself struggling against the rest of the meta (like Feraligatr or Mandibuzz), Mud Shot is the better "all-around" move because it allows Gastrodon to actually participate in the shield-baiting game.

Gastrodon's Actual Fast Moves
As of Season 26, Gastrodon only has access to two Fast Moves:

Mud-Slap (Ground)

Hidden Power (Various types)

Since Hidden Power is almost never viable in competitive PvP due to its terrible energy generation, Mud-Slap is your only real choice, even with the recent nerf.

Why the confusion happens
Gastrodon is often compared to Quagsire and Whiscash. Those two do run Mud Shot, which allows them to spam Charge Moves. Because Gastrodon is stuck with Mud-Slap, it plays very differently:

Gastrodon: High-damage "bruiser." It wins by chunking opponents down with heavy-hitting fast moves.

Whiscash/Quagsire: "Spammers." They win by reaching their Charge Moves (like Scald or Stone Edge) incredibly fast.

The Verdict for your Bastiodon Matchup
Since you have to run Mud-Slap, the nerf to its damage from 12 → 11 definitely hurts, but it doesn't change Gastrodon's role. It still absolutely deletes Bastiodon because of that double-weakness. You will just notice it takes maybe one or two more "slaps" to finish the job than it did last season.

If you find the post-nerf Mud-Slap feels too slow or clunky for your team, you might want to look at Whiscash as a replacement—it uses the Mud Shot playstyle you were asking about!
