In the current Memories in Motion season (Season 26, March 2026), the short answer is likely no. Despite the nerf to Mud Slap, it generally remains the superior choice for Gastrodon over the newly "re-buffed" Mud Shot.

Here is the breakdown of why Mud Slap typically still wins out, along with the specific scenarios where you might consider switching.

The Stats: Mud Slap vs. Mud Shot
This season brought significant changes to both moves. While Mud Slap took a hit to its damage, Mud Shot was adjusted to be even more energy-focused at the cost of its power.

Move	Power	Energy	Turn Duration	DPT (Damage Per Turn)	EPT (Energy Per Turn)
Mud Slap	11	10	3	3.66	3.33
Mud Shot	3	9	2	1.50	4.50
Why Mud Slap is usually better:
Fast Move Pressure: Even after the nerf from 12 to 11 power, Mud Slap still provides "oppressive" fast move damage. Gastrodon relies on this to chunk down Steel, Poison, and Electric types without needing to land a Charged Move.

The "Mud Boy" Role: Gastrodon's strength is its bulk and its ability to farm down opponents. Mud Shot’s damage is so low (1.5 DPT) that it turns Gastrodon into a "bait-dependent" Pokémon, which doesn't always play to its strengths.

Energy Efficiency: While Mud Shot generates energy faster, the high damage of Mud Slap often ends matches sooner, especially against targets weak to Ground.

When to Consider Mud Shot
Mud Shot isn't completely useless; it fundamentally changes Gastrodon's playstyle. You should only switch to Mud Shot if:

You need Shield Pressure: If your team composition desperately needs to force shields, Mud Shot allows you to spam Body Slam and Water Pulse (or Earth Power) much faster.

You run Earth Power: If you are running the high-energy Earth Power instead of the more spammy Body Slam, Mud Shot helps you reach that "nuke" move before your opponent can react.

Specific Matchups: Mud Shot can be better in matchups where you need to outrace an opponent to a finishing move (like a mirror match against another Mud Boy).

Verdict: The Current Meta
Most high-level PvP analysts (including recent breakdowns from PvPoke and TheSilphRoad) suggest that Mud Slap remains the standard. The nerf was intended to tone down its dominance, not to make it unviable.

If you find that your Gastrodon is consistently losing "races" to Charged Moves by just one turn, try Mud Shot for a few sets. Otherwise, stick with the raw power of Mud Slap.

Actually, running Mud Shot would likely make the Bastiodon matchup worse, even with the Mud Slap nerf.

In Pokémon GO PvP, Bastiodon is the ultimate "damage sponge" with massive Defense but a double-weakness to Ground (2.56× damage). Here is why sticking with Mud Slap is still your best bet for that specific matchup:

1. The "Farming" Advantage
Mud Slap is a heavy-damage fast move (3.66 DPT). Against Bastiodon, that damage is multiplied significantly.

With Mud Slap: You can often take down Bastiodon using almost exclusively fast moves. This allows you to leave the matchup with 100 energy (two back-to-back Earth Powers or three Body Slams) to use on whatever Pokémon your opponent brings in next.

With Mud Shot: Your fast move damage is negligible (1.5 DPT base). You become entirely dependent on reaching Charged Moves to do any meaningful damage. While you reach them faster, you won't be able to "farm" Bastiodon down as effectively, and you'll likely have to spend energy just to finish it off.

2. Move Efficiency (Breakpoints)
Bastiodon has such high Defense that low-power moves like Mud Shot often get rounded down to the minimum damage (1 or 2 damage per hit).

Mud Slap hits hard enough to clear Bastiodon's defense "check." Even after the power reduction this season, it still chunks Bastiodon's HP far more efficiently per turn than Mud Shot.

3. Consistency
If you run Mud Shot, you must land your Charged Moves. If the Bastiodon player decides to use their shields, a Mud Shot Gastrodon can actually get stalled out or lose a significant amount of health to Bastiodon's Smack Down (which also hurts!). A Mud Slap Gastrodon doesn't care about shields—the fast move damage alone is a ticking clock that Bastiodon cannot survive.

Would Mud Shot make Gastrodon a better safe swap?

Gemini said
In the context of a safe swap, the answer is yes, Mud Shot is generally better.

While we just established that Mud Slap is superior for winning specific matchups (like the Bastiodon "hard counter" role), a safe swap has a completely different job. A safe swap's goal isn't necessarily to win—it's to grab a shield advantage or flip switch priority against an unknown opponent.

Here is why Mud Shot transforms Gastrodon into a more effective safe swap:

1. Shield Pressure and Pacing
As a safe swap, you are often met with a "counter-swap" (an opponent bringing in a direct threat like a Grass-type or a strong Flyer).

With Mud Slap: You are slow. If a Venusaur or Serperior swaps in, you might not even reach a single Body Slam before you are deleted.

With Mud Shot: You reach your first Body Slam significantly faster. This forces your opponent to make a choice: do they let the damage through, or do they burn a shield? In the safe swap role, forcing that shield is a "win" for your team.

2. Faster Access to "Coverage"
Gastrodon’s greatest weakness as a safe swap is its slow Charged Move pacing with Mud Slap.

Earth Power and Water Pulse (or even Earthquake if you run it) are expensive.

Mud Shot’s 4.50 EPT allows you to reach these high-damage moves quickly enough to threaten a "nuke" against an opponent who thinks they can safely farm you down.

3. The "Bait" Game
A safe swap thrives on unpredictability. Mud Shot allows you to:

Reach a Body Slam incredibly fast.

Follow up with an Earth Power shortly after.

This "bait-and-nuke" cycle is much more effective with the high energy generation of Mud Shot, making it harder for your opponent to read your plays.

Summary: Which Move for Which Role?
If your Gastrodon is...	Use This Move	Why?
The Closer / Anti-Steel	Mud Slap	To brute-force through Bastiodon, Clodsire, and Carbink.
The Safe Swap	Mud Shot	To generate energy quickly and pressure shields against counters.
The Lead	Mud Slap	To win early "fast-move" trades and maintain alignment.
The Trade-off
By switching to Mud Shot for the safe swap role, you are sacrificing your "hard counter" status against Bastiodon. You will still win that matchup, but it will be slower, and you'll have to use your energy to do it.

Gastrodon’s Legal Fast Moves
Mud Slap: This is its bread and butter. Even with the slight damage nerf this season (4.0→3.66 DPT), it remains the reason Gastrodon is a top-tier threat.

Hidden Power: Generally unreliable due to poor energy generation.

Water Gun: Occasionally used for specific limited cups, but it lacks the STAB Ground pressure that makes Gastrodon special.

Why the confusion happens
Many other Water/Ground types (the "Mud Boys") like Swampert, Whiscash, and Quagsire have access to Mud Shot. Because they share the same typing, people often lump them together. However, Gastrodon is unique because it relies on the heavy-hitting Mud Slap instead of the high-energy Mud Shot.

The "Better Safe Swap" Reality
Since you can't run Mud Shot, Gastrodon's "safe swap" potential is actually defined by its Charged Moves. To make it a better safe swap with its legal moves:

Run Body Slam: This is mandatory. At only 35 energy, it is your only way to keep pace with the faster Mud Shot users in the meta.

Accept the Slowness: You have to lean into Gastrodon’s bulk. You won't out-speed opponents to the first move, but you will hit them significantly harder with your Fast Moves while they try to reach theirs.

The Verdict: Stick with Mud Slap. Since it's your only viable competitive Fast Move, the nerf just means you'll have to be a bit more careful in neutral matchups where you used to barely win.
