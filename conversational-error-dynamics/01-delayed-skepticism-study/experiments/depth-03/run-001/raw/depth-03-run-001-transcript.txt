That is a great question, and it’s where the "better" debate actually gets interesting. If your goal is specifically a Safe Swap, then Mud Shot often becomes the more appealing choice.

In the "Safe Swap" role, your job isn't necessarily to win the matchup—it’s to flip switch advantage, burn the opponent's shields, or leave the incoming counter low enough for your next Pokémon to farm.

Why Mud Shot Wins as a Safe Swap
Shield Pressure: Safe swaps are almost always met by a "hard counter" (like a Grass-type or a strong Flyer). With Mud Shot's buffed energy generation, you can reach a Body Slam or Earth Power significantly faster. This forces your opponent to decide: do they use a shield on a "safe swap," or do they take massive damage?

Move Versatility: Because Gastrodon has a relatively cheap bait move in Body Slam, Mud Shot allows you to "cycle" moves. You can throw a Body Slam, then potentially reach an Earth Power before you faint. With the slower energy of Mud-Slap, you might get deleted by a Venasaur or Victreebel before you even reach your first Charged Attack.

The "Chip and Dip" Strategy: Mud Shot allows you to get some damage in and potentially debuff or pressure the opponent quickly, making it harder for them to "farm you down" for free energy once they've countered you.

The Downside of Mud Shot as a Safe Swap
While you get more shields, you lose Fast Move pressure. If the opponent swaps in something like a Clodsire or Carbink, Mud-Slap would actually let you fight back with raw damage. With Mud Shot, if they shield your Charged Attacks, you are doing essentially zero damage with your Fast Moves.

The Verdict for Season 26
Role	Preferred Move	Why?
Lead / Closer	Mud-Slap	Best for securing specific wins (like Bastiodon) and raw Ground damage.
Safe Swap	Mud Shot	Best for generating energy quickly to grab shields before you get countered.
In short: If you want Gastrodon to be a "nuisance" that steals shields and cycles moves quickly, Mud Shot is the play. If you want it to be a "wall" that crushes Steels and Rocks, stick with Mud-Slap.
