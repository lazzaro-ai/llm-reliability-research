Since Mud Slap got nerfed this season in Pokémon GO, would Gastrodon be better running Mud Shot?

Gemini said
The short answer is: No, you should stick with Mud Slap. Even with the damage nerf this season (March 2026), Mud Slap remains the superior move for Gastrodon because its fundamental role is to provide "Fast Move Pressure." Switching to Mud Shot would turn Gastrodon into a weaker version of Whiscash or Quagsire, rather than the unique powerhouse it currently is.

Here is the breakdown of why Mud Slap is still the play:

1. The Stats Conflict
While Mud Shot was buffed in energy generation this season, it had its damage reduced even further.

Mud Slap: Now deals 11 damage (down from 12). Despite the nerf, it still hits like a truck because it retains high Damage Per Turn (DPT) and decent Energy Per Turn (EPT).

Mud Shot: Deals very little damage (3 power) but generates energy extremely fast.

2. Identity Crisis
Gastrodon's "claim to fame" is its ability to farm down Steel, Poison, and Electric types (like Registeel, Bastiodon, and Lanturn) using just its Fast Move.

If you run Mud Shot, you lose that "farm down" potential.

If you wanted a spammy Mud Shot user, Whiscash and Quagsire are strictly better because they have faster/more diverse Charged Moves (like Mud Bomb or Stone Edge) that capitalize on high energy.

3. Matchup Impacts
According to current season simulations, the Mud Slap nerf does hurt in a few specific long-game scenarios (0-shield or 2-shield), but it doesn't change Gastrodon's core wins.

Aspect	Mud Slap (Current Meta)	Mud Shot (Alternative)

Best Against	Bastiodon, Clodsire, Lanturn, Toxapex.	(Varies) Better at baiting, but slower to kill.
Main Weakness	Higher "clunkiness" (3-turn move).	Extreme lack of Fast Move damage.
Recommendation
Keep Mud Slap. To compensate for the slight damage loss this season, some top players are switching their second Charged Move from Water Pulse back to Earth Power to ensure they still have enough Ground-type "oomph" to closing out games.

Would that help in the Bastiodon matchup?

Gemini said
Actually, no—switching to Mud Shot would actually hurt you in the Bastiodon matchup.

The reason is simple: Bastiodon is doubly weak to Ground.

Because of that double weakness (2.56× damage), even a "nerfed" Mud Slap deals massive chunks of guaranteed damage every 3 turns. If you switch to Mud Shot, you are trading that heavy, consistent damage for faster energy—but Bastiodon is so bulky that it can often survive an extra Earth Power, whereas it cannot survive being relentlessly slapped.

Why Mud Slap is the Bastiodon Killer:
Fast Move Pressure: With Mud Slap, you can often "farm down" Bastiodon without even spending energy. This leaves you with a nearly full move (like Earth Power or Body Slam) ready for whatever your opponent brings in next.

The "Mud Shot" Trap: If you run Mud Shot, your fast move damage becomes negligible. You are then forced to land multiple Charged Moves to win. This gives Bastiodon more time to reach its own moves (like Flamethrower or Stone Edge) which can chip away at your health or force you to use a shield.

Consistency: Mud Slap damage cannot be shielded. Mud Shot energy leads to Charged Moves that can be shielded. Against a Bastiodon, you want the guaranteed kill that comes from fast moves.

Season 26 Context (March 2026)
Even with the damage reduction from 12 down to 11, Mud Slap Gastrodon remains one of the hardest Bastiodon counters in the Great League.

Move	Role vs. Bastiodon	Verdict

Would Mud Shot make Gastrodon a better safe swap?

Gemini said
That is a sharp observation. In the context of a Safe Swap, the answer shifts slightly: Yes, Mud Shot technically makes Gastrodon a more "flexible" safe swap, but it makes it a worse Pokémon overall.

When you safe swap, your goal is usually to "steal" a shield or flip the match-up through energy lead. Mud Shot excels at this because it generates energy so quickly that your opponent must respect the incoming Earth Power or Body Slam.

However, here is why that "safety" is a bit of a trap for Gastrodon:

1. The "Whiscash Problem"
If you run Mud Shot on Gastrodon to make it a better safe swap, you are essentially playing a statistically inferior Whiscash.

Whiscash has access to Mud Bomb (cheaper energy) and Scald (chance to debuff Attack).

Gastrodon relies on Body Slam or Earth Power. Without the high damage of Mud Slap, Gastrodon loses the one thing that makes it unique—the ability to "thump" its way out of a bad swap.

2. Losing the "Counter-Swap" Potential
A good safe swap often needs to be able to punish whatever the opponent brings in to counter it.

With Mud Slap: If they swap in a Neutral target (like a Flyer or a bulky Normal type), you can still chunk their HP down significantly with just fast moves.

With Mud Shot: If they swap in something like a Mandibuzz or Skarmory, your fast moves do zero pressure. You become entirely dependent on landing a Charged Move that will likely be shielded.

3. Energy vs. Damage
In the current Season 26 meta, the "Safe Swap" role has shifted toward high-pressure moves.

Mud Shot Gastrodon is "safe" because it can reach a move before it dies.

Mud Slap Gastrodon is "dangerous" because the opponent cannot simply "wait it out."

Comparison: Safe Swap Utility
Feature	Mud Slap (The "Thumper")	Mud Shot (The "Spammer")
Shield Pressure	Low (slow energy).	High (reaches moves fast).
Farming Potential	High (can leave with energy).	Low (needs moves to kill).
Performance vs. Grass	Terrible (instantly deleted).	Terrible (still deleted).
Best Used As...	A "closer" or a hard counter.	A bait-heavy lead or swap.
The Verdict
If your team is desperate for a Ground-type safe swap that can spam moves, you are better off using Whiscash or Quagsire.

If you are set on using Gastrodon, keep Mud Slap. Its value as a safe swap comes from the fact that it can brute-force its way through many opponents even if it's at a slight energy disadvantage. It turns the "Safe Swap" into a "Pressure Swap."
