Here is the breakdown of the situation for the 2026 meta:

1. The Stat Changes
Niantic has been tweaking the "Mud" moves to differentiate their roles. Here is where they stand now:

Move	Type	Damage (DPT)	Energy (EPT)	Role

2. When to keep Mud-Slap
Even with the nerf from 4.0 to 3.66 DPT, Mud-Slap remains a very strong move. It is now a clone of Fire Spin rather than Astonish.

Farm-down Potential: If you face a lot of Steel, Electric, or Poison types (like Bastiodon, Toxapex, or Clodsire), Mud-Slap allows you to "farm them down" with fast move damage alone, saving your energy for the next Pokémon.

Consistency: It provides steady, heavy chip damage that doesn't rely on getting a shield bait with Body Slam.

The Verdict: Keep Mud-Slap if your team needs Gastrodon to act as a hard counter to specific meta threats where fast-move pressure is more valuable than speed.

3. When to switch to Mud Shot
With Mud Shot being reverted to its high-energy generation (4.5 EPT) this season, it has become the "standard" choice again for many Mud Boys.

Charge Move Spam: If you want to maximize Body Slam spam or reach Earth Power as fast as possible, Mud Shot is the superior choice. This is especially useful if you find yourself in neutral matchups where you need to outpace the opponent to win.

Shield Pressure: Mud Shot forces the opponent to use shields much earlier in the match.

The Verdict: Switch to Mud Shot if you are using Gastrodon as a Safe Swap or if your playstyle relies on "shield baiting" and firing off multiple Charged Attacks.

Would Mud Shot make Gastrodon a better safe swap?

In the current Season 26 meta, switching to Mud Shot does indeed make Gastrodon a more traditional and versatile Safe Swap, but it changes the "threat profile" of the Pokémon significantly.

Here is the breakdown of why Mud Shot is often preferred for the Safe Swap role, and what you sacrifice to get it.

Why Mud Shot is the "Safe" Choice
As a safe swap, your goal is usually to either win switch advantage or leave the opponent so low on health/shields that your next Pokémon can easily farm them down.

Faster "Body Slam" Spam: With Mud Shot’s high energy generation (4.5 EPT), you reach your first Body Slam in just 4 seconds (4 Mud Shots). This allows you to chip away at counters and pressure shields before they can ramp up their own damage.

Neutral Matchup Flipping: In matchups where you don't deal super-effective damage (like against Azumarill or Sableye), Mud Shot allows you to outpace them to your Charged Moves. Mud-Slap is often too slow in these "neutral slogs" to be effective as a swap-in.

The "Mud Boy" Standard: Running Mud Shot brings Gastrodon closer to the playstyle of Whiscash or Quagsire. It makes Gastrodon feel more "fluid" and less like a slow, heavy-hitting tank.

The Trade-off: What you lose
While Mud Shot is "safer," it makes you much less "deadly" in specific scenarios:

Feature	Mud-Slap (The Specialist)	Mud Shot (The Generalist)
Mirror Match	Wins (The fast move damage wins the race)	Loses to Mud-Slap variants

The Verdict for Season 26
If you are using Gastrodon as a Closer or a Lead to punish specific Steel/Poison types, stick with Mud-Slap. The raw damage is still high enough to make those matchups effortless.

However, if you are using it as a Safe Swap, Mud Shot is the better choice. It prevents you from being "farmed down" by a counter-swap (like a Grass type) quite as easily, as you'll likely be able to fire off two Body Slams or an Earth Power before going down, either taking a shield or leaving a massive dent.
