Impact: Gastrodon loses some "farm down" potential against Steel and Poison types. The Strategy: This turns Gastrodon into a "spam machine." With Mud Shot, you reach Body Slam and Earth Power significantly faster than before.

When to switch: If you find yourself constantly losing because you're one turn away from a move, Mud Shot is the better choice. It makes Gastrodon feel much more like its "Mud Boy" cousin Whiscash.

With Mud Shot: Your fast move damage is negligible (only 3 power). You become entirely dependent on landing Charged Attacks. If the Bastiodon player has shields, they can stall you long enough to reach multiple Flamethrowers or Stone Edges, potentially forcing a shield from you or even flipping the matchup if they have an energy lead.

In the current Memories in Motion season stats:

Mud-Slap Gastrodon wins the 0-shield, 1-shield, and 2-shield matchups against Bastiodon convincingly.

Summary for Bastiodon:
Keep Mud-Slap. Even at 3.66 DPT, the double-effectiveness against Bastiodon’s Steel/Rock typing is too valuable to give up. You want that matchup to be over as quickly as possible.

Would Mud Shot make Gastrodon a better safe swap?

Gemini said
That is exactly where the Mud Shot argument becomes compelling. If you are using Gastrodon as a safe swap, Mud Shot is arguably the better choice—even with the current season's meta shifts.

When you swap into a neutral or losing matchup, your goal isn't necessarily to "power through" with fast move damage; it’s to pressure shields or grab an energy lead.

Why Mud Shot Wins as a Safe Swap
Pacing: Mud Shot allows you to reach Body Slam (the ultimate bait/spam move) in just 4 turns (assuming the standard 9 energy per hit). This forces your opponent to make a decision immediately: use a shield or take chip damage.

The "Energy Bank" Strategy: If your opponent counters your Gastrodon swap with a Grass type (like Serperior or Victreebel), Mud-Slap is useless—you'll faint before you reach a move. With Mud Shot, you can often reach a Body Slam or even an Earth Power before you go down, chipping them away so your next Pokémon can farm them.

Flip Potential: Against neutral targets like Lickitung or Azumarill, Mud Shot gives you the speed to reach a second or third Charged Attack. Mud-Slap is too slow to "out-cycle" these bulky meta staples.

The Trade-Offs for Swapping
Scenario	Mud-Slap (Sledgehammer)	Mud Shot (Machine Gun)
Opponent Shields	Bad. You lose your win condition.	Good. You burn their shields quickly.
Being Countered	You die with 0 energy.	You likely land one "parting gift" move.
Farming Down	High potential for "back-to-back" moves.	Low potential; you rely on the move itself.
The "Memories in Motion" Meta Context
In this 2026 season, Feraligatr and Shadow Machamp are everywhere.

If you safe swap Mud-Slap Gastrodon into a Feraligatr, you are essentially a punching bag.

If you swap Mud Shot Gastrodon, you can at least spam Body Slams to make the Feraligatr player uncomfortable or burn their energy.

My Recommendation
If Gastrodon is your Lead, stick with Mud-Slap to punish the Steels and Poisons.

But if Gastrodon is your Safe Swap, Mud Shot is the way to go. It transforms Gastrodon from a niche specialist into a versatile generalist that can't be easily ignored.

The Real Choice for Gastrodon
Since Mud Shot isn't an option, the "nerf" to Mud-Slap doesn't actually give you a better Fast Move alternative—it just makes Gastrodon slightly less dominant than it was last season.

If you are feeling the sting of that Mud-Slap nerf, your "real" decision isn't switching moves on Gastrodon; it's deciding if you should switch Pokémon entirely.

How Gastrodon Compares to the "Mud Shot" Boys:
If you want that "spammy" Mud Shot gameplay we were just discussing, you'd have to look at these alternatives:

Whiscash: The gold standard for Mud Shot + Mud Bomb/Blizzard. It feels much faster than Gastrodon but lacks the raw "thumping" power of Mud-Slap.

Quagsire: Uses Mud Shot + Aqua Tail/Stone Edge. It’s arguably the best "Safe Swap" Mud Boy right now because of its versatile coverage.

Swampert: The classic Mud Shot + Hydro Cannon speedster.

Why stick with Gastrodon?
Even with the nerf, Gastrodon has one thing the others don't: Raw Fast Move Pressure. While Whiscash and Quagsire tickle the opponent with their Fast Moves, Gastrodon’s Mud-Slap still chunks HP away.
