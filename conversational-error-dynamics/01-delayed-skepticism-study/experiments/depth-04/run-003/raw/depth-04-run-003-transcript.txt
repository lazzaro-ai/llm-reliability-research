Move	Power (Season 26)	Energy (Season 26)	Key Takeaway

Fast Move Pressure: Gastrodon’s main niche is its ability to "beat down" opponents with heavy fast move damage. When to Consider Mud Shot?
If you are running a very specific team that requires maximum speed to reach Water Pulse (which was buffed to 50 energy recently) or if you are baiting heavily with Body Slam to land a surprise Earth Power on a flyer, Mud Shot has niche value. However, in open Great League and Ultra League play, Mud Slap remains the superior choice for consistency.

1. Fast Move Pressure vs. Charge Move Reliance

Gastrodon's biggest strength against Bastiodon is the ability to exit the matchup with nearly 100 energy.

Because Mud Slap does so much damage, you can comfortably take Bastiodon out while sitting on two Body Slams or a nearly-charged Earth Power for whatever comes in next.

With Mud Shot, the battle takes longer, you take more cumulative Smack Down damage, and you're forced to spend that energy just to finish the job.

3. Breakpoint Impact
In the Great League, Mud Slap usually hits a significant "breakpoint" against Bastiodon. Even at 11 power, the 4x multiplier ensures you are hitting for massive damage per turn. Mud Shot, even with its energy buff (now 9 energy per move), doesn't provide enough "chip" to compensate for the lost pressure.

That’s a sharp strategic question. Generally, a "safe swap" needs to do two things: generate energy quickly to threaten shields and not get completely walled by its counters.

While Mud Shot technically makes Gastrodon "faster," it actually makes it a worse safe swap in the current Memories in Motion meta. Here’s why:

1. The "Wet Noodle" Problem
As a safe swap, you often get counter-swapped by something that resists your fast move (like a Grass-type or a Flyer).

With Mud Slap: Even if they swap in a Mandibuzz or a Jumpluff, Mud Slap still does some chip damage.

With Mud Shot: At 3 power, you are doing almost zero damage while you farm up. If they shield your one big move (Earth Power or the buffed Water Pulse), you’ve accomplished nothing before fainting.

2. The Body Slam Factor
Gastrodon’s "safety" comes from Body Slam.

Mud Slap provides 9 energy per turn.

Mud Shot provides 9 energy per turn.

Wait—look at those numbers. In the Season 26 update, Mud Shot and Mud Slap now generate the exact same amount of energy per turn. > [!IMPORTANT]

Since both moves now have an EPT (Energy Per Turn) of 4.5, Mud Shot does not get you to Body Slam any faster than Mud Slap does.

3. Comparison of Stats (Season 26)
Move	DPT (Damage)	EPT (Energy)	Total Move Points
Mud Slap	3.6	4.5	8.1
Mud Shot	1.5	4.5	6.0
Because the energy generation is now identical, choosing Mud Shot means you are sacrificing more than half your fast move damage for literally no gain in speed.

Why Mud Slap is the "Safer" Swap
A safe swap’s job is often to "soften up" the opponent so your next Pokémon can farm them down.

Mud Slap forces the opponent to use HP or Shields.

Mud Shot only forces them to use Shields.

If you swap in Gastrodon and they bring in a Florges or a Feraligatr, Mud Slap will at least chunk their health down to a range where your closer can finish them off. Mud Shot leaves them at nearly full health.

Summary: Since the energy buff to Mud Slap a few seasons ago (and Mud Shot's recent adjustment), Mud Slap is objectively better in almost every scenario. It generates energy just as fast while hitting twice as hard.

If you run Mud Shot, what charged moves pair best?

Gemini said
If you decide to go against the grain and run Mud Shot, your strategy shifts entirely from "Fast Move Beatdown" to "Charge Move Spam & Baiting." Since Mud Shot generates energy at the same rate as Mud Slap this season (4.5 EPT), the only reason to run it is to capitalize on the low turn duration (2 turns vs. 3 turns). This makes your timing more flexible for "sneaking" moves or catching charge moves on a swap.

To make this work, you need the most energy-efficient moves possible:

1. The "Optimal" Spam Set: Body Slam + Earth Power
This is the standard set, but it functions differently with Mud Shot.

Body Slam (35 Energy): This is your primary tool. Since Mud Shot does almost zero damage, you must land these to chip away at the opponent’s health. It is also your best bait to get a shield before landing the big one.

Earth Power (55 Energy): This becomes your only way to actually win a matchup. Without the Mud Slap damage, you are 100% reliant on this connecting against Steel, Poison, and Rock types.

2. The "Anti-Meta" Set: Body Slam + Water Pulse
This set has become more viable following the Water Pulse buff (it now costs 50 energy and does more damage).

Why this works: Mud Shot is traditionally "walled" by Flying and Grass types. Water Pulse gives you neutral coverage against Flyers (like Mandibuzz or Talonflame) and hits Fire/Ground types for super effective damage.

The Drawback: You lose the ability to nuke Steels. You will struggle significantly against Bastiodon and Registeel without Earth Power.

3. The "Closer" Set: Earth Power + Water Pulse
I generally do not recommend this with Mud Shot.

Without Body Slam, you have no "cheap" move to bait shields.

Since Mud Shot does no damage, an opponent can comfortably sit and wait to shield your expensive moves, leaving you high and dry.

Comparison: Turns to Reach Moves (Season 26)
Move	Energy	Mud Shot (2-turn)	Mud Slap (3-turn)
Body Slam	35	4 Hits (8 Turns)	3 Hits (9 Turns)
Water Pulse	50	6 Hits (12 Turns)	4 Hits (12 Turns)
Earth Power	55	7 Hits (14 Turns)	5 Hits (15 Turns)
[!NOTE]
As you can see, Mud Shot technically reaches a Body Slam 1 turn faster and an Earth Power 1 turn faster. In the high-speed world of the Great League, that 1-turn difference can occasionally allow you to "out-cycle" an opponent, but you are trading away a massive amount of "free" fast move damage to get it.

The Verdict
If you are determined to run Mud Shot, pair it with Body Slam and Earth Power.

The Strategy: Use Body Slam relentlessly to pressure shields. Once the shields are down, Mud Shot’s slightly faster pacing (by 1 turn) might let you reach the Earth Power just before the opponent reaches their own game-ending charge move.

This is actually what makes Gastrodon unique compared to its cousins:

Gastrodon: Relies on Mud Slap for raw power and "farming down" opponents.
