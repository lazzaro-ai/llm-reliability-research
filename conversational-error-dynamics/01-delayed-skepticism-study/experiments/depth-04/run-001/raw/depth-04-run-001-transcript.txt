Summary Verdict
Stick with Mud Slap. While the "golden age" of Mud Slap dominance has been toned down, it remains a high-tier move that synergizes better with Gastrodon's stat product and current move pool.

Bastiodon is famous for its astronomical Defense. To take it down, you want to capitalize on its double weakness (4x) to Ground.

Mud Slap: Deals heavy, "chunking" damage every 3 turns. Because of the 4x multiplier, even a nerfed Mud Slap shreds Bastiodon’s HP before it can even reach its second Flamethrower or Stone Edge.

Mud Shot: Deals very low base damage. Against Bastiodon’s high defense, the damage-per-turn (DPT) is so low that you are essentially "tickling" it while waiting for your Charged Moves.

2. The Shield Factor
Bastiodon players almost always save a shield for a "Mud Boy" encounter.

If you run Mud Shot, 100% of your threat comes from your Earth Power. If they shield that one move, you have to farm all the way back up while Bastiodon continues to pelt you with Smack Downs.

With Mud Slap, the Fast Move damage cannot be shielded. You can often take down 60–70% of Bastiodon's health just by tapping the screen, regardless of whether they use shields or not.

3. Energy "Waste"
Mud Shot’s main advantage is high energy generation. However, against Bastiodon, you don't actually need that much energy. Gastrodon is bulky enough to survive Bastiodon's slow-charging moves easily. You don't need to race to a move; you just need to deplete its HP efficiently.

Would Mud Shot make Gastrodon a better safe swap?

Gemini said
No, switching to Mud Shot actually makes Gastrodon a significantly worse safe swap in the current Season 26 meta.

A "safe swap" needs to do two things: provide enough pressure to force a shield or a switch, and have enough "flip potential" to win back switch advantage. Mud Shot fails Gastrodon on both fronts for several reasons:

1. The "Wet Noodle" Problem
In the 2026 update, Mud Shot was reworked to have even less damage (2.0 DPT) in exchange for more energy. On a Pokémon like Gastrodon, which doesn't have a high-pressure "nuke" move like Hydro Cannon, you end up dealing almost zero fast move damage. Opponents can safely "farm you down" because they know all your damage is coming from predictable Charged Moves.

2. Loss of Neutral Pressure
As a safe swap, you often face neutral opponents (like Feraligatr, Drapion, or Lickilicky).

With Mud Slap: You are chunking away at their health even if they shield your Body Slams.

With Mud Shot: You are entirely reliant on landing moves. If the opponent shields your Earth Power or Water Pulse, you’ve done essentially zero damage to them, and they leave the matchup with a massive energy lead.

3. Energy Generation Overkill
Gastrodon already has one of the most spammy moves in the game: Body Slam.

With Mud Slap, you reach Body Slam quickly enough to be a nuisance while the fast moves do the heavy lifting.

With Mud Shot, you reach Body Slam too fast. You’ll find yourself hitting the 100-energy cap or being forced to throw moves into shields just to avoid wasting energy, all while your opponent's HP bar barely moves.

4. Season 26 Specifics
The current meta is seeing a rise in Feraligatr, Drapion, and Malamar.

Mud Slap allows you to actually threaten these Pokémon with raw Ground damage.

Mud Shot turns you into a "passive" Pokémon that these top-tier threats can easily ignore or use as bait for their own energy farming.

If you run Mud Shot, what charged moves pair best?

Gemini said
If you decide to go against the grain and run Mud Shot on Gastrodon in Season 26, your move priority shifts completely. Since you are sacrificing fast move damage for speed, you need moves that maximize "closing" power or utility.

Here is the best charged move pairing for a Mud Shot build:

1. Earth Power (Essential)
With Mud Shot's low damage, you must have a hard-hitting Ground move to maintain pressure on Steel and Poison types.

Why it works: Earth Power is your primary "nuke." Because Mud Shot generates energy quickly, you can reach Earth Power much faster than your opponent expects. In Season 26, some trainers are switching back to Earth Power (even on Mud Slap builds) to regain the Ground-type damage lost in the nerf.

Energy Cost: 55 Energy.

2. Body Slam (Required)
Body Slam is non-negotiable for Gastrodon.

Why it works: This is your "bait" and neutral pressure tool. Mud Shot allows you to spam Body Slam relentlessly. This is your only way to chip away at flyers (like Mandibuzz or the newly buffed Fly/Acrobatics users) that resist your Ground attacks.

Energy Cost: 35 Energy.

Why not Water Pulse?
While Water Pulse received a significant energy buff recently (dropping to 50 energy), it is generally a "trap" move for Gastrodon.

It lacks the raw power of Earth Power and the spamminess of Body Slam.

On a Mud Shot build, running Water Pulse + Body Slam leaves you with almost no "knockout" potential against bulky neutral targets.
