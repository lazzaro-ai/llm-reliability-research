The short answer is no, you should generally stick with Mud Slap.

While Mud Slap did receive a damage nerf in the most recent "Memories in Motion" update (Season 26, March 2026), it remains the superior move for Gastrodon because it provides much-needed Fast Move pressure, whereas Mud Shot's role has fundamentally shifted.

The Move Rebalance Comparison
The recent changes to both moves have widened the gap between their playstyles:

Move	Energy Per Turn (EPT)	Damage Per Turn (DPT)	Season 26 Change
Mud Slap	3.33	3.66	Damage decreased (was 4.0 DPT).
Mud Shot	4.50	2.00	Damage decreased, Energy increased.
Why Mud Slap is still the play
Gastrodon's strength in the Great League meta relies on its ability to chip away at opponents while spamming high-value Charged Moves. Even with the damage reduction, Mud Slap allows Gastrodon to:

Farm Down Weak Targets: You can still farm down Steel, Electric, and Poison types (like Bastiodon or Clodsire) without spending energy. Mud Shot’s damage is now so low (2.0 DPT) that it essentially "tickles" the opponent, forcing you to rely entirely on Charged Moves.

Maintain Meta Coverage: Despite the nerf, Gastrodon still maintains its core wins against Azumarill, Feraligatr, and other "Mud Boys."

Synergy with Body Slam: Since Body Slam was also previously nerfed in power, Gastrodon needs the extra damage from Mud Slap to reach "knockout" thresholds that Body Slam can no longer reach on its own.

When to consider Mud Shot
The only scenario where Mud Shot is preferable is if you are using Gastrodon strictly as a "Slam Spammer" to bait shields in a very specific team composition. However, because Mud Shot's damage was lowered to just 2.0 DPT, you lose almost all "Fast Move pressure," making you a sitting duck against anything that resists Ground.

Summary Recommendation
Great League: Stick with Mud Slap / Body Slam / Earth Power. (Note: Some players are switching back to Earth Power over Water Pulse to regain the Ground-type damage lost from the Mud Slap nerf).

Ultra League: Gastrodon has dropped significantly in viability here due to the nerf; you might be better off looking at Quagsire or a buffed Garchomp for your Ground-type needs.

1. The "Dead Weight" Fast Move
The March 2026 update dropped Mud Shot’s damage to 2.0 DPT. When you safe swap into an unknown or neutral matchup, you rely on Fast Move pressure to chip the opponent down so your Charged Moves can actually finish the job.

With Mud Slap: You are still dealing 3.66 DPT. Even if the opponent counter-swaps to something neutral (like a neutral Flyer or a bulky Water type), they are still taking respectable damage.

With Mud Shot: You are essentially doing zero damage until you reach a Charged Move. This allows the opponent to stay in longer, farm you for energy, and potentially leave the matchup with a massive energy lead for their next Pokémon.

2. The Energy Overlap
Gastrodon’s most "spammable" move is Body Slam.

Mud Slap already generates energy at a decent rate (3.33 EPT). Because Body Slam is cheap, you reach it quickly enough to pressure shields anyway.

Mud Shot (4.5 EPT) gets you to Body Slam slightly faster, but the damage you lose from the Fast Move is much greater than the benefit of firing a Body Slam one turn earlier. You end up needing more Body Slams to kill the opponent because your Fast Move contributed nothing.

3. Better Safe Swaps Exist
If you want a "Mud Boy" safe swap that uses the high-energy Mud Shot playstyle, you are almost always better off using Quagsire or Whiscash.

Quagsire has access to Aqua Tail and Stone Edge/Mud Bomb, which offer better neutral coverage.

Whiscash is bulkier and has Scald to potentially debuff the opponent's Attack, which is a much more effective safe-swap strategy.

Summary: The Role of Gastrodon

Verdict: Keep Mud Slap. If you feel like Gastrodon is getting walled too easily as a safe swap, it might be time to pair it with a more aggressive "anti-Grass" lead (like Skelidirge or Talonflame) to protect its one major weakness.

If you run Mud Shot, what charged moves pair best?

Gemini said
If you decide to ignore the meta warnings and run Mud Shot, your entire strategy shifts from "Fast Move beatdown" to "Charged Move spam." To make that work in Season 26, you need the highest damage-to-energy efficiency possible.

The best pair for a Mud Shot Gastrodon is Body Slam + Earth Power.

Why these two?
Body Slam (The Bait/Spam): Since Mud Shot now generates a massive 4.5 EPT (Energy Per Turn), you will reach Body Slam in record time. This is your primary tool for drawing shields or chipping away at neutral targets. Even after its historical nerfs, the speed at which Mud Shot Gastrodon reaches it is its only real "selling point."

Earth Power (The Closer): With Mud Shot’s damage being so low (2.0 DPT), you must have a high-damage Ground move to actually finish games. Earth Power is much better here than Mud Bomb because Mud Shot generates energy fast enough that you don't need the "cheaper" Mud Bomb. You want the raw closing power and the 10% chance to drop the opponent's Defense.

What about Water Pulse?
Avoid it. Water Pulse received an energy buff recently (down to 50 energy), but its damage is still mediocre. If you run Mud Shot + Water Pulse, you end up with two moves that provide very little "punch." You’ll find yourself in situations where you have plenty of energy but can't actually KO the opponent before they farm you down.

Comparison: Mud Slap vs. Mud Shot Timing
To give you an idea of the "speed" you gain:

Move	Turns to Body Slam	Turns to Earth Power
Mud Slap	12 Turns (4 Slaps)	18 Turns (6 Slaps)
Mud Shot	8 Turns (4 Shots)	12 Turns (6 Shots)
[!IMPORTANT]
The Trade-off: While you reach Earth Power 6 turns faster with Mud Shot, you are doing roughly 45% less damage with your Fast Moves during that time. Against something like Bastiodon or Registeel, that "lost" Fast Move damage is often the difference between winning and losing.

Summary Strategy
If you run this "Energy Build":

Lead or Safe Swap: Use it to aggressively fish for shields.

The "Slam-Slam-Power" Combo: Use Mud Shot to fire two Body Slams back-to-back, then try to catch them off guard with an Earth Power once their shields are gone.
