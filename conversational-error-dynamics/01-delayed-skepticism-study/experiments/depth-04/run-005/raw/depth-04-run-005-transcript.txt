Since Mud Slap got nerfed this season in Pokémon GO, would Gastrodon be better running Mud Shot?

Gemini said
It’s a great question, and the answer is a classic "it depends on your team," but for most players, Mud Slap is still the way to go. While the Season 26 (March 2026) "Memories in Motion" update did lower Mud Slap’s damage (from 12 down to 11), it also significantly buffed Mud Shot’s energy generation (now 9 energy per turn) while lowering its damage. Here is the breakdown of why you might stick with Mud Slap or make the swap.

The Stats Comparison
Move	Damage Per Turn (DPT)	Energy Per Turn (EPT)	Playstyle
Mud Slap	3.66 (Nerfed)	3.33	"Farm down" pressure; heavy fast move damage.
Mud Shot	1.50	4.50 (Buffed)	"Spam" pressure; reaches Charged Moves incredibly fast.
Why Mud Slap is Usually Still Better
Even with the nerf, Gastrodon’s identity in the current meta is built around its ability to chunk down opponents.

The "Mud Boy" Advantage: Gastrodon is unique because it combines high bulk with the heavy damage of Mud Slap. If you switch to Mud Shot, you become a "spam-only" Pokémon like Swampert or Whiscash, but without the devastating Hydro Cannon or the utility of Scald/Mud Bomb.

Neutral Matchups: Mud Slap allows Gastrodon to beat things just by tapping, which is vital in the Great League against Steel, Poison, and Fire types. With the nerf, you lose some "edge case" wins (like Lickilicky or Shadow Feraligatr in the 2v2 shield), but you still dominate your core targets.

When to Consider Mud Shot
You should only switch to Mud Shot if your team strategy relies entirely on Shield Pressure.

Earth Power Nukes: If you run Earth Power, Mud Shot gets you there significantly faster. This can catch opponents off guard if they expect the slow build-up of Mud Slap.

Safe Swapping: If you use Gastrodon as a safe swap to bait shields with Body Slam, Mud Shot's 4.5 EPT is terrifying. You can fire off Body Slams so quickly that the opponent is forced to burn shields or take constant chip damage.

The Verdict
Keep Mud Slap if you want Gastrodon to be a consistent, hard-hitting Ground-type threat. The damage nerf is noticeable, but it hasn't "killed" the Pokémon; it just brought it back in line with other meta picks.

Pro Tip: If you feel like you're missing that extra Ground-type "oomph" after the nerf, try swapping Water Pulse for Earth Power. It helps reclaim some of the KO power you lost on the fast move.

Would that help in the Bastiodon matchup?

Gemini said
Actually, running Mud Shot would likely make the Bastiodon matchup worse, or at the very least, much more stressful.

While the Season 26 buff to Mud Shot (up to 4.5 EPT) lets you reach your Charged Moves faster, Bastiodon is one of the few Pokémon where you absolutely crave raw Fast Move damage. Here is why:

1. The "Farm Down" Factor
Bastiodon is double-weak to Ground (4× damage). Even with the slight nerf to Mud Slap’s power (from 12 to 11), it still chunks Bastiodon for massive damage every two turns.

Mud Slap: You can often "farm down" a Bastiodon using only your Fast Move, leaving you with 100 energy to unleash on whatever comes in next.

Mud Shot: Since Mud Shot’s damage was nerfed (down to 3 power), it barely tickles Bastiodon. You would be forced to use your Charged Moves to win the fight, leaving you energy-dry for the next opponent.

2. Shielding Scenarios
With Mud Slap: You don't care if Bastiodon shields. Your Fast Move damage is what’s doing the heavy lifting. You can comfortably save your own shields because Bastiodon’s Smack Down damage is neutral, while your Mud Slap is devastating.

With Mud Shot: You are completely reliant on Earth Power or Water Pulse connecting. If the Bastiodon player successfully baits you or shields your Earth Power, the match becomes surprisingly close because your Fast Move isn't providing any "insurance" damage.

3. Breakpoints
Bastiodon is famous for its astronomical Defense. The Mud Slap nerf means you might lose a "breakpoint" (the specific amount of damage dealt per hit) depending on your Gastrodon’s IVs. However, even a nerfed Mud Slap deals significantly more "damage per second" to a Steel/Rock type than the current high-energy, low-damage Mud Shot.

Summary for the Bastiodon Matchup:
Stick with Mud Slap. It ensures that Bastiodon remains a "free win" and a source of energy for you. If you switch to Mud Shot, you turn a dominant matchup into a "shield-guessing game" that you don't need to play.

Would Mud Shot make Gastrodon a better safe swap?

Gemini said
In short: Yes, Mud Shot makes Gastrodon a significantly better safe swap. While Mud Slap is a "bully" move that wins specific matchups (like the Bastiodon one we discussed), Mud Shot turns Gastrodon into a "utility" piece. A safe swap's job isn't necessarily to win the match—it’s to flip switch advantage or, at the very least, leave the opponent with zero shields and a sliver of health.

Here is why the Season 26 Mud Shot buff (4.5 EPT) changes the math for Gastrodon as a safe swap:

1. Shield Pressure is King
When you swap Gastrodon into a neutral or losing lead, you want to force the opponent to react.

Mud Slap (Slow): If you swap in with Mud Slap, the opponent can often "out-pace" you. They might reach two Charged Moves before you reach your first Earth Power.

Mud Shot (Fast): You reach Body Slam in just 4 turns (assuming the 2-turn move cadence). That is blistering speed. You can often fire off two Body Slams before most meta threats can even get to their first big nuke. This forces them to either burn shields early or lose a chunk of HP to spam.

2. The "Nuke" Threat
The biggest buff to Gastrodon’s safe-swap potential is the threat of a "blind" Earth Power.
Because Mud Shot generates energy so quickly now, your opponent has to respect the Ground-type nuke almost immediately. If they swap in a counter like Clodsire or Lickilicky, they are forced to shield your first move because they can't be sure if it's a Body Slam bait or a full-power Earth Power.

3. Surviving the "Counter-Swap"
If you safe-swap Gastrodon and your opponent brings in a Grass-type (the hard counter), Mud Shot gives you a fighting chance to actually do something:

With Mud Slap: You likely die before reaching a single move.

With Mud Shot: You can almost always reach one Body Slam or even a Water Pulse (for neutral damage) before you faint. That chip damage can be the difference between your next Pokémon being able to farm them down or getting swept.

The Trade-Off (The "Cost" of Mud Shot)
Feature	Mud Slap Swap	Mud Shot Swap
Energy Lead	Poor (slow generation)	Excellent (can leave with a move ready)
Closing Power	High (fast move pressure)	Low (reliant on landing moves)
Consistency	Predictable	Dynamic / Bait-heavy
My Recommendation
If your team is "Top Heavy" (meaning your other two Pokémon are slow and hard-hitting), use Mud Shot on Gastrodon to provide the speed and shield pressure they lack.

However, if you are running a "Fast" team already (like with a Feraligatr or Greninja), you might actually prefer Mud Slap to maintain some heavy-hitting bulk.

If you run Mud Shot, what charged moves pair best?

Gemini said
If you’re committing to the Mud Shot life, you are pivoting from a "brawler" to a "spammer." To make that work, you need moves that maximize that high energy generation (4.5 EPT) to either bait shields or land heavy-hitting "nukes."

Here are the two best pairings for a Mud Shot Gastrodon:

1. The Standard "Spam & Nuke" Pairing
Move 1: Body Slam (35 Energy)

Move 2: Earth Power (55 Energy)

This is the most consistent set for a safe swap.

The Logic: Mud Shot reaches a Body Slam in just 4 turns (8 turns total, since it's a 2-turn move). This is incredibly fast. You use Body Slam to chip away at the opponent’s health or bait a shield.

The Threat: Once you've baited a shield, you go for the Earth Power. Earth Power benefits from the Same Type Attack Bonus (STAB) and hits significantly harder than Gastrodon's other options. With Mud Shot, you can reach two Earth Powers faster than almost any other Ground-type in the Great League.

2. The "Anti-Meta" Utility Pairing
Move 1: Body Slam (35 Energy)

Move 2: Water Pulse (60 Energy)

This is a niche pick, but it has grown in popularity since the Season 26 update.

The Logic: Even though Water Pulse is generally a "bad" move (low damage for high energy), it provides crucial coverage against Flying types (like Mandibuzz or Gliscor) and Fire types (like Talonflame) that resist your Ground moves.

The Strategy: Since Mud Shot generates energy so quickly, the "clunkiness" of Water Pulse is mitigated. You can actually reach it in a reasonable timeframe to flip a matchup that a Ground-only Gastrodon would lose.

Which one should you choose?
Feature	Earth Power Set	Water Pulse Set
Best For	Closing games / Winning shields	Safe swapping / Flexibility
Key Targets	Clodsire, Registeel, Carbink	Talonflame, Mandibuzz, Skeledirge
Risk Factor	Gets walled by Flying/Grass	Lower overall damage output
[!IMPORTANT]
Why not Earth Power + Water Pulse?
You almost always need Body Slam. Without it, Gastrodon becomes too "expensive" to run. You'll find yourself fainting with 45 energy—not enough for your big moves, but more than enough for a Body Slam that could have flipped the match.

The Final Verdict
If you want the "best" version of Mud Shot Gastrodon, go with Body Slam + Earth Power. It punishes the Steel and Poison types that are currently everywhere in the meta while forcing shields early and often.
