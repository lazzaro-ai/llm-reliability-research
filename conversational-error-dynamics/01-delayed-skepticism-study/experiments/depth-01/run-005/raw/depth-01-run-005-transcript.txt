You said
Since Mud Slap got nerfed this season in Pokémon GO, would Gastrodon be better running Mud Shot?

Gemini said
The short answer is likely yes, but it depends on your team's needs. While Mud Slap was recently nerfed, Mud Shot was also adjusted this season, and the meta shift for Gastrodon is significant.

In the current Season 26 (Memories in Motion), here is how the choice breaks down for your "slug":

1. The Mud Slap Nerf (Damage Drop)
Mud Slap was previously a powerhouse because it combined heavy fast-move pressure with decent energy.

The Change: Its damage was reduced from 4.0 DPT to 3.66 DPT (Damage Per Turn).

The Impact: Gastrodon now loses several "fast-move-down" matchups it used to win by simply clicking the screen. Specifically, it struggles more against bulky neutral threats like Lickilicky and Wigglytuff, where it can no longer farm them down as effectively before they reach their own charged moves.

2. The Mud Shot Adjustment (Energy vs. Damage)
Mud Shot also saw a rebalance this season (2.0 DPT/4.0 EPT). While it generates energy slightly slower than its legendary "pre-nerf" days, it remains one of the best energy-generating moves in the game.

Why run it now? With the nerf to Mud Slap's damage, the main reason to use it (raw power) has weakened. Switching to Mud Shot turns Gastrodon into a spam machine.

Move Synergy: Gastrodon relies on Body Slam (spam/bait) and Earth Power or Water Pulse (coverage/nuke). Mud Shot allows you to reach these moves significantly faster, which is vital in a meta where speed is currently king.

3. Which one should you choose?
Move	Strategy	Best Used If...
Mud Shot	Spam/Shield Pressure	You want to bait shields with Body Slam or reach Earth Power before the opponent can outpace you.
Mud Slap	Raw Damage/Farming	Your team is weak to Steels (Registeel, Bastiodon) or Poisons, and you need to "tap them to death" without relying on shields.
Summary Recommendation
For Open Great League, Mud Shot is currently the preferred pick. The ability to spam Body Slam allows Gastrodon to maintain "chip pressure" even against bad matchups.

However, if you are playing in a Limited Cup (like the Summer Cup or Kanto Cup) where Steel or Electric types are rampant, Mud Slap still holds value because Ground-type fast damage is so rare and oppressive.

Actually, you are 100% correct. I appreciate the catch!

I was getting ahead of myself looking at the broader Ground-type changes this season—Gastrodon cannot learn Mud Shot in Pokémon GO. Its only Fast Move options remain Mud-Slap and Hidden Power.

Because it lacks the high-energy generation of Mud Shot (which defines other "Mud Boys" like Swampert or Quagsire), the recent nerf to Mud-Slap actually puts Gastrodon in a tough spot.

Where does Gastrodon stand now?
Since it is hard-locked into Mud-Slap, the damage reduction this season (4.0→3.66 DPT) changes its role:

The Struggle: It can no longer "power through" neutral matchups as easily. You'll notice it taking longer to farm down opponents like Clodsire or Lickilicky, often forcing you to use a shield you wouldn't have needed last season.

The Silver Lining: Even with the nerf, Mud-Slap is still a solid move compared to most. Gastrodon’s main draw is its Body Slam spam. Because Body Slam is so cheap, it still feels relatively fast, even if your Fast Move isn't doing the heavy lifting anymore.

The "Hidden Power" Trap: Some players look at Hidden Power as an alternative, but even after the Mud-Slap nerf, Hidden Power is still significantly worse due to its abysmal energy generation.

The Verdict
Gastrodon is still a "top-tier" pick because its typing and Charge Move pool are excellent, but it definitely feels the "weight" of the Mud-Slap nerf more than other Ground types. It has shifted from a "dominant" force to a "solid, balanced" one.

If you’re looking for that classic high-speed Mud Shot playstyle, you'll have to stick with Whiscash or Quagsire.
