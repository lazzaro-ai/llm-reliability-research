1. The Power vs. Energy Trade-off
Gastrodon is a "Mud Boy" that relies on its bulk to farm down opponents.

Mud Slap: Even with the nerf, it deals heavy damage (DPT) while maintaining decent energy generation (EPT). It allows you to "farm down" opponents like Clodsire, Toxapex, and Steel-types without needing to spend shields on charged moves.

Mud Shot: This move was also tweaked recently (Power 4 → 3, Energy 8 → 9). While it generates energy faster, Gastrodon’s charged move pool (Body Slam and Earth Power/Water Pulse) doesn't benefit enough from the extra speed to justify losing the massive raw damage of Mud Slap.

2. Matchup Impacts
The Mud Slap nerf mainly affects Gastrodon’s ability to "brute force" neutral matchups. You might notice it now struggles more in the 2v2 shield scenario against:

Lickilicky and Wigglytuff (formerly narrow wins, now closer to ties or losses).

Feraligatr and Shadow Swampert (the reduced damage makes the "farm down" race tighter).

3. Recommended Moveset
If you feel the loss in damage, the best adjustment isn't switching your fast move, but rather looking at your Charged Moves:

Fast Move: Mud Slap

Charged Move 1: Body Slam (Essential for baiting and spam)

Charged Move 2: Earth Power (Consider running this over Water Pulse to reclaim some of the Ground-type damage lost from the Mud Slap nerf).

The Verdict: Stick with Mud Slap. In a meta filled with Poison and Steel types (like the current Spring Cup), the raw "slap" damage is too valuable to give up for the slightly faster pacing of Mud Shot.
